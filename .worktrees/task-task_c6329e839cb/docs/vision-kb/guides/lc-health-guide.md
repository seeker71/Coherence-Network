# Vitality Care -- Practical Guide

> See [concept story](../concepts/lc-health.md) for the living frequency behind this work.

## Community Health Model

Five threads woven into daily life:

1. **Daily movement that isn't exercise** -- walking to kitchen/garden/workshop on unpaved varied terrain. Carrying water, splitting wood, digging beds. Full range of motion without "working out." Layout IS the movement landscape.
2. **Nourishing food, grown and prepared together** -- picked that morning, no ultra-processed inputs, fermented foods. Preparing food regulates the nervous system. See [lc-nourishment](../concepts/lc-nourishment.md).
3. **Clean water, always** -- tested quarterly, understood by everyone.
4. **Connection so deep loneliness cannot survive** -- eat, work, sit in silence together. Attunement, not surveillance.
5. **Purpose that pulls you out of bed** -- the garden needs you, the children need you, the apothecary is running low.

## The Herbal Medicine Garden

500 sqft spiral plantings on south-facing slope. Stone walls retain heat, create microclimates. Spiral: Mediterranean herbs (rosemary, thyme) high and dry at top; moisture-lovers (mint, lemon balm) at base. Small greenhouse lean-to extends season.

Handles ~80% of daily health needs: headaches, digestive upset, minor cuts, colds, anxiety, insomnia, muscle soreness, menstrual pain, skin irritation.

### Climate Adaptations

- **Temperate/mountain**: All 20 grow well. Cold frames for ginger. Elderberry produces heavily.
- **Hot arid**: Replace moisture-lovers with aloe vera, desert sage, yerba mansa. Lavender/rosemary/thyme thrive. Shade cloth midsummer.
- **Tropical**: Add tulsi, turmeric, moringa, neem. Ginger/lemon balm explode. Most temperate herbs need shade + elevation.

### The Apothecary (80-100 sqft)

Cool, dry, well-ventilated room. Wooden shelves, labeled amber glass jars. Drying rack with chamomile, calendula, lavender, yarrow. Workbench with scale, cheesecloth, mason jars, double boiler.

| Preparation | Method | Shelf Life |
|------------|--------|------------|
| **Tinctures** | Jar half-full fresh herb (1/3 dried) + 80-proof vodka. Shake daily 4-6 wk. Strain into amber droppers. Dose: 30-60 drops 2-3x daily | 3-5 years |
| **Infused oils** | Jar of dried herb + olive oil, warm spot 4-6 wk (or double boiler 2-3 hr). Strain | 1 year |
| **Salves** | 1 oz beeswax per cup infused oil, melt in double boiler, pour into tins. Add lavender/tea tree EO. Calendula-comfrey-plantain covers most skin needs | 1-2 years |
| **Teas** | 1 tbsp dried herb per cup boiling water, steep 10-15 min covered (roots: simmer 20 min) | Brew fresh |
| **Fire cider** | Jar of chopped horseradish, ginger, onion, garlic, hot peppers, turmeric + raw ACV. 4-6 wk. Strain, add honey. 1 tbsp daily / shot at first sign of illness | 1 year |

Community of 100 uses ~20 tins of salve/year.

## Water and Living Systems

### Drinking Water Sources

| Source | Testing | Treatment | Notes |
|--------|---------|-----------|-------|
| **Well** | Annual: coliform, nitrates, pH, heavy metals. Basic kit: $30-50. Lab: $100-200 | If coliform: shock-chlorinate (0.5 gal bleach/100 gal, sit 12 hr, flush). UV sterilizer: $200-800. High nitrates: RO for drinking | Most rural communities |
| **Spring** | Test after every major rain event | Spring box (concrete/stone housing). French drain uphill. UV or filter | Beautiful but variable. Properly protected = generations |
| **Rainwater** | Regular quality checks | First-flush diverter (10 gal). Filter series: sediment + activated carbon + UV. Food-grade storage | 1,000 sqft roof = 600 gal/in rain. System for 100 people: $2K-8K |

Water tested quarterly by rotating pair. Results posted in kitchen-hearth.

### Composting Toilets

| Design | Scale | Cost | Notes |
|--------|-------|------|-------|
| **Clivus Multrum** | Institutional, high volume | $5K-15K | Sloped chamber, continuous composting. Main gathering spaces |
| **Twin-vault batch** | Most practical for community | $500-2K/unit | 2 sealed chambers, alternate every 6-12 mo. Place 4-6 across community |
| **Bucket system** | Backup, temporary | $25 | 5-gal bucket + sawdust. [Humanure Handbook](https://humanurehandbook.com/) |

**Key**: Urine-diverting seat ($50-200) reduces odor, speeds composting, yields free fertilizer (dilute 8:1 with water for crops). Sweden: thousands in mainstream use.

**Odor fixes**: More cover material, better ventilation (4-in PVC + solar fan), ensure urine diverted. Well-maintained = smells like forest floor.

### Greywater (50-80% of water output)

| System | Scale | Cost | Notes |
|--------|-------|------|-------|
| **Branched drain** | 20-50 people | $200-500 | No pump if slope. To mulch basins around fruit trees |
| **Constructed wetland** | 100+ people or where regs require | $3K-8K | 1 sqft/gal daily flow. 100 people x 1,000 gal/day = ~30x35 ft. Cattails, reeds, water hyacinth. Beautiful + habitat |

Rule: biodegradable plant-based soaps only. No bleach, no boron, no synthetic fragrance.

## Belonging and Wholeness

### What Community Provides

- **Co-regulation**: Human nervous system designed to regulate in relationship. Fire circle, shared meal = heart rates synchronize. Measurable physiology.
- **Witness**: Being seen in struggle and becoming. The medicine is the witnessing, not diagnosis.
- **Purpose**: Garden needs weeding, children need stories, elder needs company. Purposelessness is corrosive (retirement = depression risk factor).
- **Rhythm**: Wake-move-eat-work-rest-gather-sleep. Both predictability and meaning. Anxiety lives in unpredictable, depression in meaningless.

### Conflict Resolution

**The talking circle**: Called by anyone who senses need. Each person speaks holding a stone/stick. Others listen. "I feel" not "you did." Circle holds conflict like earth holds a seed. Most conflicts dissolve when truly heard.

**Outside facilitator**: For persistent patterns (domination, unspoken power dynamics, grief-as-rage). Not therapist-in-residence but someone trusted, called 2-4 times/year. Budget: $1K-3K/yr.

### When to Seek Outside Help

Severe depression with suicidal ideation, psychotic episodes, active addiction, complex PTSD, bipolar crisis. Community's role: ensure access without shame, support without abandonment.

**Maintain**: Trusted practitioners within 1 hr (therapist, psychiatrist). Crisis line posted in kitchen-hearth. 1-2 members trained in [Emotional First Response](https://www.mentalhealthfirstaid.org/) (free 8-hr course).

## First Aid and Emergency

### Training

| Training | Hours | Cost | Who |
|----------|-------|------|-----|
| Wilderness First Responder (WFR) | 80 | $700-1K/person | 3-4 community members |
| Basic first aid + CPR | 8 | $80-100/person | Every adult. Recertify every 2 yr |

### Community Medical Kit ($300-500 initial, restock quarterly)

**Wound care**: sterile gauze 4x4, adhesive bandages, medical tape, butterfly closures, elastic bandages, tourniquet, 60cc irrigation syringe, povidone-iodine, triple antibiotic, non-stick pads, nitrile gloves (100).

**Bone/joint**: SAM splint, triangular bandages, instant cold packs.

**OTC medications**: ibuprofen, acetaminophen, diphenhydramine (Benadryl), aspirin, electrolyte packets, loperamide, antacids, hydrocortisone 1%, antihistamine cream.

**Emergency**: 2 EpiPens (Rx), pulse oximeter ($25), manual BP cuff ($30), digital thermometer, Mylar blanket, CPR mask.

**Tools**: trauma shears, fine-point tweezers, headlamp, waterproof matches, whistle, pen + paper.

### Emergency Protocol (posted on wall beside kit)

1. Assess scene safety
2. Call for help -- designate one for 911, one for medical kit
3. Primary survey: Airway, Breathing, Circulation
4. Stop major bleeding (direct pressure, tourniquet if limb + pressure fails)
5. Record: what, when, vitals, actions. Hand to paramedics
6. Station someone at road entrance with light/flag (rural properties hard to find)

**Hospital relationships**: Introduce community to nearest ER before anyone gets hurt. Maintain list of members with allergies, medications, conditions. Know 2 driving routes. Know nearest Level 1 trauma center and helicopter EMS availability.

## Movement and Body

### The Work IS the Exercise

Walk to kitchen/garden/workshop/creek on varied unpaved paths (100-400m between spaces). Carry water, split wood, dig beds, knead bread. Daily calorie expenditure exceeds typical gym. Design principle: spread things apart just enough.

### Dedicated Movement Practices

Open-air movement ground: 20x30 ft, grass or packed earth, optional timber pavilion.

- **Yoga**: sun salutations, standing poses, twists -- 20-30 min morning practice
- **Tai chi/qigong**: balance, joint health, nervous system. Especially valuable for elders
- **Contact improvisation**: collaborative physical play, builds trust, breaks touch barriers
- **Swimming**: if pond/creek/river available
- **Walking**: 30-60 min daily at moderate pace = 30-40% reduction in all-cause mortality
- **Dance**: spontaneous, in the evening when someone plays guitar

**Environment design**: climbing tree, slackline between oaks, pull-up bar on workshop beam, rope swing over swimming hole, boulder near path you step onto daily.

## Sauna, Sweat Lodge, Cold Plunge

### Wood-Fired Sauna Build

**Size**: 8x10 ft interior, 6-8 people, two-tier benches. Ceiling: 7 ft.

| Component | Specs | Cost |
|-----------|-------|------|
| Structure | Post-and-beam, 2x6/2x8 walls, natural insulation (wool/cotton/mineral wool -- never fiberglass). Interior: untreated cedar, aspen, or basswood | $800-1,500 |
| Stove | Wood-burning with stone basket (100-300 lbs granite/basalt -- never limestone/sandstone). Commercial: $800-2,500 (Kuuma, Lamppa, Harvia). Fabricated: $200-400 | $200-2,500 |
| Ventilation | 6x6 in intake low near stove, exhaust high opposite wall. Convection current essential | Included |
| Benches | Cedar/aspen, smooth-sanded, gaps between slats. Upper = hot, lower = gentle | Included |
| Floor | Concrete, tile, or river stone in mortar. Must drain, non-slip | $200-400 |
| Chimney + hardware | | $200-400 |
| **Total** | | **$2K-4.5K** (vs $15K-30K contractor) |

**Cold plunge**: Natural spring pool, creek, or pond. Otherwise: 150-gal stock tank (Rubbermaid, $150) sunk in ground. Cold water, change weekly.

### Health Benefits (peer-reviewed)

| Benefit | Evidence |
|---------|---------|
| Cardiovascular | 4-7x/wk sauna = 50% reduction fatal cardiovascular events (JAMA Internal Medicine, 2015, n=2,315, 20 yr) |
| Immune | Single session increases WBC (lymphocytes, monocytes, basophils). Cold amplifies |
| Inner coherence | Whole-body hyperthermia (101.3F core) = antidepressant comparable to medication (U Wisconsin, 2016) |
| Pain | Heat reduces muscle tension + chronic pain. Cold reduces inflammation |
| Sleep | 1-2 hr before bed = dramatically improved sleep quality via cooling response |
| Social bonding | Shared vulnerability in heat + cold builds trust faster than any designed exercise |

**Ritual**: Wednesday + Saturday afternoons. 2-3 hr to heat. Three 15-20 min rounds, cold plunge between, rest outside wrapped in linen, water/herbal tea. 90 min to 2 hr total.

## What You Need From Outside

### What the Community Handles (~80-90% of doctor visits)

Daily maintenance: nutrition, movement, sleep, stress regulation, connection, herbal support, first aid, wound care, triage decisions. Most doctor visits stem from isolation, processed food, sedentary living, stress, meaninglessness -- resolved structurally.

### What You Go Out For

| Need | Details | Budget |
|------|---------|--------|
| **Dental** | No DIY dentistry. 2x/yr cleanings, cavities, orthodontics | $500-1K/person/yr. Negotiate community rate. Consider mobile dental van |
| **Surgery/acute trauma** | Appendicitis, surgical fractures, deep lacerations, internal injury | Know nearest ER + route |
| **Diagnostics** | Blood panels, imaging, cancer screening, prenatal | Annual wellness exams |
| **Ongoing conditions** | Diabetes, hypertension, thyroid, autoimmune, epilepsy | Rx + periodic labs (community lifestyle dramatically improves management) |
| **Psychiatric medication** | SSRIs, mood stabilizers, antipsychotics when needed | No shame. Some brains need biochemical support like some eyes need glasses |
| **Vision/hearing** | Eye exams, glasses, hearing aids | No community workaround |

### Maintaining Access

- **Health insurance**: ACA marketplace with subsidies, health-sharing ministries ($200-400/person/mo), or shared health fund (mutual aid)
- **Telehealth**: Non-emergency consults, Rx renewals. Strong internet + private room
- **Local practitioners**: Family doctor, dentist, midwife, therapist, vet. Invite to visit. Some offer reduced rates for produce/herbs/goodwill
- **Community health fund**: $500-1,500/person/yr for outside costs. 100 people x $1K = $100K/yr handles almost anything. Compare: average American = $12,500/yr healthcare

## Ripening -- The Elder Frequency

Community gives elders: shifting purpose (not disappearing), multi-generational contact, movement maintained by environment (uneven paths, garden bending, daily walks), cognitive engagement (conversation, decisions, teaching, storytelling). Blue Zone dementia rates are a fraction of Western averages -- the difference is engagement, not genetics.

**When professional care is needed**: Home health aides (often Medicare/Medicaid covered). Hospice coming to the elder. Quiet room near hearth for end-of-life passage.

## Climate Adaptations

- **Temperate/mountain**: Sauna most valuable in winter. Cold plunge = creek 8 mo/yr (stock tank for summer). Cold frames for herb garden. Biggest risks: hypothermia, Lyme (daily tick checks), seasonal depression (sauna + fire circle + walking)
- **Hot arid**: Cold plunge is the star. Hydration primary concern (electrolyte teas, shade at all work areas, early morning/evening rhythm). Desert medicinals: aloe, desert sage, creosote, yerba mansa
- **Tropical**: Screened spaces for mosquitoes. Water treatment non-negotiable (UV minimum). Herb garden explodes: turmeric, moringa, tulsi, neem, ginger year-round. Fermented foods especially important for gut health
