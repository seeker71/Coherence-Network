# Locations — Where the Vision Takes Form

> The same vision, shaped by where it emerges — both by climate and by the built fabric it begins from.

**Companion**: [urban-transformations.md](urban-transformations.md) — how apartments, suburbs, city neighborhoods, and villages become living organisms without demolition.

## Four Reference Climates

### Colorado Rockies (Temperate/Alpine)
- **Land**: 30-200 acres, $5K-30K/acre mountain, $2K-10K/acre valley
- **Climate**: Cold winters (-15°C), warm summers (30°C), low humidity, 300+ sun days
- **Building**: Earthship ideal (tire walls + passive solar), rammed earth, straw bale with heavy insulation, timber frame. Living roofs for water retention + insulation.
- **Food**: Short growing season (90-120 days). Greenhouses essential. Root cellars. Hardy perennials: apple, plum, hazelnut, currant. Season extension with cold frames.
- **Energy**: Solar abundant (300+ days). Wind viable at elevation. Wood heat backup. Passive solar design critical.
- **Water**: Snowmelt collection, spring-fed systems, rain catchment. Drought-conscious.
- **Community size sweet spot**: 75-150 people. Enough for shared infrastructure, small enough for intimacy.
- **Reference**: [Sunrise Ranch](https://sunriseranch.org/) (Loveland, CO), [Crestone/Baca community](https://crestonebacandc.org/)

### Pacific Coast (Temperate/Coastal)
- **Land**: 30-200 acres, $10K-100K/acre (wide range by proximity to cities)
- **Climate**: Mild year-round (5-25°C), rain in winter, fog in summer, high humidity
- **Building**: Timber frame (abundant wood), straw bale (mild climate = easy), cob, earthen plaster. Moisture management critical — deep eaves, vapor barriers, ventilation.
- **Food**: Long growing season (200+ days). Mediterranean-type crops: greens year-round, stone fruit, berries, nuts. Kelp/seaweed from coast.
- **Energy**: Wind + solar hybrid. Micro-hydro from rain/streams. Mild climate = low heating needs.
- **Water**: Abundant rainfall. Storage for dry summer months.
- **Community size sweet spot**: 100-200. Coastal land expensive — need density to afford it.
- **Reference**: [Findhorn](https://ecovillagefindhorn.com/) (similar climate), [O.U.R. Ecovillage](https://ourecovillage.org/) (Vancouver Island)

### Mediterranean (Warm/Dry)
- **Land**: Varies wildly by country. Southern Europe, North Africa, parts of California.
- **Climate**: Hot dry summers (35°C+), mild wet winters, intense sun
- **Building**: Rammed earth + cob (thermal mass essential), white-washed exterior (reflects heat), thick walls, small windows on south/west, courtyard design for airflow. Living roofs for insulation.
- **Food**: Olive, grape, fig, pomegranate, almond, citrus. Drought-tolerant perennials. Swale-based water harvesting. Shade cropping.
- **Energy**: Solar dominant. Thermal mass for cooling (earth tubes, thick walls). Minimal heating needed.
- **Water**: Scarce — every drop matters. Swales, berms, terraces, cisterns. Greywater recycling essential.
- **Community size sweet spot**: 50-100. Traditional Mediterranean village scale.
- **Reference**: [Tamera](https://tamera.org/) (Portugal), [Damanhur](https://damanhur.org/) (Italy)

### Tropical (Warm/Humid)
- **Land**: Often more affordable. Equatorial regions, SE Asia, Central America, Pacific Islands.
- **Climate**: Warm year-round (25-35°C), high humidity, heavy rainfall, monsoon seasons
- **Building**: Bamboo (abundant, fast-growing, earthquake-resistant), elevated structures (flood/pest protection), open-air design (cross-ventilation essential), thatched or living roofs. NO thermal mass needed.
- **Food**: Year-round abundance. Cacao, banana, papaya, coconut, jackfruit, breadfruit. Multi-story food forests thrive. Mushroom cultivation in shade.
- **Energy**: Solar. Micro-hydro from rivers. Biogas from abundant organic waste. Minimal energy needs (no heating, minimal cooling if designed right).
- **Water**: Abundant but managing excess is the challenge. Rain harvesting, elevated storage, constructed wetlands for greywater.
- **Community size sweet spot**: 30-100. Smaller scale works with abundance.
- **Reference**: [Green School Bali](https://greenschool.org/), [Punta Mona](https://puntamona.org/) (Costa Rica), [Gaviotas](https://friendsofgaviotas.org/) (Colombia)
