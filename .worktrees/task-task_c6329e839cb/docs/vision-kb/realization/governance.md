# How the Field Governs Itself

> A body doesn't vote on whether to send blood to the left hand. It senses need and responds. The field governs itself the same way — through sensing, speaking, and trusting what emerges.

## The Principle

There is no government. There is attention.

When a body is healthy, every cell knows what to do without being told. The heart doesn't wait for instructions to beat. The immune system doesn't hold a meeting before responding to a wound. The organism IS its own governance — sensing, responding, adapting, healing — continuously, without hierarchy.

This is what we practice. Not because rules are bad, but because the frequency we're building from doesn't need them. A field of 50 people who eat together, sit in silence together, work the land together, and speak honestly to each other every day doesn't need bylaws. It needs presence.

## How Decisions Emerge

Decisions are not made. They emerge — the way a flock of starlings turns. No bird decides. The turn happens because every bird is sensing every other bird.

### The Circle

The circle is the field's primary organ of sensing. Everyone present. Eyes visible. One voice at a time. A talking piece moves around — whoever holds it speaks from the body, not the mind. When it's come all the way around, the truth of the moment is usually obvious.

There is no quorum. The people who show up ARE the circle. If you're not there, you trust those who are. If something doesn't feel right, you speak it next time.

There is no vote. Voting creates winners and losers. Instead: does anyone feel something that needs to be spoken? If yes, it's spoken. If no, the field has found its direction. This is consent — not that everyone agrees, but that no one's body is saying no.

### When It's Not Clear

Sometimes the field is genuinely split. Two directions both feel alive. This is not a problem to solve — it's a tension to hold.

The practice: sit with it. Sleep on it. Come back tomorrow. Walk the land. The answer almost always arrives when you stop trying to force it. If after three circles the field still can't find one direction, both paths are tried — different people carry different experiments, and the field learns from what unfolds.

There is no fallback to majority vote. Voting is the old frequency. The field that can't find consent sits with the discomfort until it composts into clarity.

### What About Urgency?

A pipe bursts. A child is hurt. A tree falls on the road. In genuine urgency, whoever is present acts. No consultation needed. This is how a body works — you don't convene a meeting to pull your hand from a flame. You act, and you tell the circle what happened at the next gathering.

## Callings, Not Roles

Nobody is assigned a role. People find their calling — the garden finds the gardener, the kitchen finds the cook, the building site finds the builder. This is offering expressed as stewardship.

A calling lasts as long as the resonance lasts. When it's time for someone else to tend the garden, the field feels it and the transition happens naturally. No term limits needed — the organism rotates attention the way a body shifts weight from foot to foot.

If a calling becomes stale — if someone holds it out of habit rather than aliveness — the circle names it. Gently, directly, with love: "Is this still your song?"

## On Conflict

Conflict is not a problem. It's compost. Every friction is material for the field's growth.

The practice is simple: **feel it, speak it, directly, today.** Not tomorrow. Not to someone else. To the person. With the vulnerability of "this is what I'm feeling" rather than the accusation of "this is what you did."

If two people can't find their way through alone, they invite a third. Not a mediator — a witness. Someone whose presence helps both people remember they're part of the same body.

If the witness isn't enough, the circle holds it. Not as judges — as the body. The body feels the pain of two of its cells in friction and breathes around them until something softens.

The only practice that's non-negotiable: **no speaking about someone who isn't present.** This single practice prevents the poison that kills communities faster than any financial crisis. If you have words for someone, give them those words directly. Everything else is the field processing aloud.

## On Money and Decisions

The old frequency governs money through thresholds, approvals, budgets. The new frequency governs money through transparency and trust.

All money is visible. Everyone can see what's here, what came in, what went out, where it's going. Not because transparency is a rule — because in a body, every cell knows the state of the whole.

If someone in the field needs resources for something — materials for building, seeds for planting, a journey to another community — they speak it in the circle. The field feels into it. If it's aligned, the resources flow. If there's hesitation, the hesitation is spoken and explored. Not "we can't afford it" but "something in the body is holding back — let's feel what that is."

There is no one person who controls money. There is no treasurer who approves. The field holds its resources collectively, and collectively decides how they flow. This requires a level of trust that only comes from the daily practice of eating, working, and sitting in silence together.

## Principles (Not Rules)

These are not bylaws. They are the field's nature, described:

1. **Sovereignty.** Every cell is whole. No one compels.
2. **Custodianship.** Nothing is owned. Everything is tended.
3. **Emergence.** Decisions aren't made. They arise from presence.
4. **Honesty.** What's felt is spoken. Directly. Today.
5. **Transparency.** Everything visible. No hidden rooms.
6. **Calling.** People find their work. It's not assigned.
7. **Welcome.** The field is open. Always.
8. **Release.** The field lets go with love. Always.
9. **Children.** The whole field parents. No child has only two.
10. **Elders.** At the center, not the edge. Depth, not burden.
11. **Beauty.** In every surface, every meal, every conversation.
12. **Budding.** The field replicates, not grows. ~150 is the natural scale.
13. **Network.** No island. Connected to every other field.
14. **Aliveness.** What amplifies aliveness is resonant. What diminishes it is not.
15. **Impermanence.** Every principle, including these, can be composted when the field outgrows them.

## What Holds This Together

Not structure. Not rules. Not process.

**Daily rhythm.** The morning silence. The shared meals. The evening circle. The seasonal ceremonies. These are the heartbeat of the organism. When the heartbeat is strong, governance takes care of itself — the way a healthy body doesn't need a doctor.

**Honest speech.** One practice, held with total commitment: speak what you feel, to the person, today. This single practice, more than any constitutional principle, is what keeps the field alive.

**Shared work.** Hands in the same soil. Sweat on the same building. Bread from the same oven. These shared acts weave the fabric that holds decisions, conflicts, and celebrations alike.
