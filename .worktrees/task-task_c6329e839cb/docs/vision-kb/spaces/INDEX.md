# Spaces — Physical Environments of the Living Collective

> Every space in the community is a living organ of the organism.

## Space Types

| Space | Concept | Purpose | Key material |
|-------|---------|---------|-------------|
| **Hearth** | lc-nourishment | Communal kitchen + dining | Cob oven, timber frame |
| **Nest** | lc-space, lc-v-shelter-organism | Private sleeping dwelling | Cob, earthship, bamboo |

| **Learning** | lc-discovery, lc-transmission | Open classroom + library | Covered outdoor, earth floor |
| **Sound** | lc-v-harmonizing | Music circle + instruments | Acoustic natural surfaces |

## Layout Principles

1. **Central gathering** — the fire circle is the heart. All paths lead there.
2. **Kitchen near garden** — nourishment flows from soil to table in minutes.
3. **Private nests on edges** — dwelling clusters at the periphery, common spaces at the center.
4. **Workshop accessible** — near materials storage, away from sleeping areas (noise).
5. **Sanctuary in nature** — silence space tucked into trees, away from activity.
6. **Water features throughout** — streams, ponds, rain collection visible and audible.
7. **Children weave everywhere** — no fenced "children's area", the whole community is their classroom.

## Reference Layouts

- [Crystal Waters](https://crystalwaters.org.au/) — 640 acres, 83 lots on 20%, 80% common land
- [Sieben Linden](https://siebenlinden.org/en/) — car-free, straw-bale, zone-based layout
- [EcoVillage at Ithaca](https://ecovillageithaca.org/) — 175 acres, 3 neighborhoods of 30-40 homes
- [One Community Global](https://onecommunityglobal.org/) — open-source village plans (CC BY 3.0)
