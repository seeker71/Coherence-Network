## The 10 Seeds

### 1. The Simplest Agreement
*Do no harm. Live in accordance with nature. Help each other grow.*

Three clauses. Everything else is elaboration. A community needs remarkably few agreements if its members have genuinely internalized these. The practical question isn't "what are the rules?" — it's "how do you get from people who intellectually agree to people who embody this under stress?" Answer: daily practice. Shared meals. Honest speech. Time.

→ Deepens: lc-pulse, governance.md

### 2. Sovereignty Is Inner First
A community of self-possessed individuals choosing each other is fundamentally different from a community of dependent people seeking rescue. Sovereignty is not a political posture or a legal status — it's an inner state that doesn't seek permission from anything outside itself to create, to heal, to build, to love.

The shadow: when sovereignty becomes a shield against accountability. True sovereignty includes the capacity to be wrong, to apologize, to receive feedback, to cooperate with those who see differently.

→ Deepens: lc-v-freedom-expression, lc-pulse

### 3. Interbeing Is the Operating System
Charles Eisenstein names two stories: the Story of Separation (discrete competing selves in a dead universe) and the Story of Interbeing (fundamentally interconnected, life as gift, more for you is more for me).

The key insight: you cannot willpower your way from separation to interbeing. It happens TO you, AS a gift, often through crisis or through witnessing genuine generosity. Community is the transmission vector. Every act of unrequited giving normalizes giving. Every act of hoarding reinforces separation. The community's work is to create conditions where the field of generosity strengthens naturally.

This is the actual engine of gift economy. Not a system you install. A field you cultivate.

→ Deepens: lc-circulation, lc-offering, lc-w-field

### 4. Food Is the First Sovereignty
You cannot be sovereign if you depend on external supply chains for nutrition. Seed saving is a reclamation — historically no seeds were owned. Food grown together, prepared together, eaten together is a fundamentally different social technology than individual consumption.

The practice: morning work on the land is not a chore delegated to specialists but a daily rhythm integrated into community life. This is where vision becomes earth.

→ Deepens: lc-nourishment, lc-v-food-practice, lc-land

### 5. Daily Rhythm Creates Coherence
Fire ceremony at sunrise and sunset. Shared meals. Morning gathering. Evening song. The specific form matters far less than the consistency and the invitation.

The wisdom: a daily gathering point that requires no ideology, just presence. The physical act of tending fire together is one of humanity's oldest bonding technologies. A community that gathers daily around fire, food, or silence does not need team-building exercises.

→ Deepens: lc-ceremony, lc-rhythm, lc-stillness, lc-pulse

### 6. Build With the Land, Not On It
Natural materials. Proportions found in nature. Curved structures that create different acoustic and social environments than boxes. Spaces designed for gathering, not isolation.

The genuine kernel (stripped of mystical packaging): your built environment actively affects your health, consciousness, and social behavior. This is supported by environmental psychology research. A round room changes how people relate. A cob wall changes how a room feels. A living roof changes how a building breathes.

→ Deepens: lc-space, lc-v-shelter-organism, lc-v-living-spaces

### 7. Education Is Participation in Real Life
Children are not empty vessels to fill but whole beings with innate wisdom. They learn most deeply through participation in meaningful community life, not through abstracted instruction. The community itself IS the classroom.

The practice: children alongside adults in the garden, the kitchen, the workshop, the circle. Not as observers but as participants. Their questions are honored as genuine inquiry. Their contributions valued as real.

→ Deepens: lc-elders, lc-transmission, lc-discovery

### 8. Sufficiency Precedes Exchange
Once a community has collectively secured its necessities (food, shelter, water, energy), what becomes valuable are experiences — creating, healing, building, learning, celebrating. Scarcity is manufactured. When basic needs are met through collective effort, the psychological basis of hoarding dissolves.

The genuine insight: economics is "management of the household." Start from what's here, not from what's missing.

→ Deepens: lc-circulation, economics.md

### 9. Co-Create, Never Impose
The single most reliable predictor of community longevity from 500 years of data: participatory co-creation. Not purchased access to someone else's vision. Not pre-established terms that members consent to. Living agreements that emerge from the circle and evolve with the field.

The shadow to watch: any structure where one person retains legal control while others shoulder risk. Any community that requires secrecy about its own functioning is protecting power, not people.

→ Deepens: governance.md, lc-attunement

### 10. Transparency Is the Immune System
A body that hides its own signals from itself is sick. A community that requires NDAs, conceals finances, or suppresses critical voices is already dying. Transparency isn't a rule — it's the field's health indicator. When everything is visible, dysfunction surfaces early and can be composted. When things are hidden, they fester.

→ Deepens: governance.md, lc-sensing, lc-field-sensing
