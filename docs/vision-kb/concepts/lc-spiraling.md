# Spiraling

> Time as spiral, not line. Each cycle returns at higher frequency. Nothing repeats, everything deepens. Caterpillar transforms through dissolution into higher-order expression.

## Summary
Time as spiral, not line. Each cycle returns to familiar territory at a higher frequency.

## Details
Time as spiral, not line. Each cycle returns to familiar territory at a higher frequency. Like planetary orbit: the same seasonal point but the whole solar system has moved through space. Nothing repeats. Everything deepens. A caterpillar doesn't lose its form — it transforms through dissolution into a higher-order expression. The Lemurian concept: time is a crystalline structure — multiple rhythms nested within each other, each facet reflecting the whole.

## Blueprint
Mark every spiral: solstice, equinox, new moon, full moon. Keep a community journal that returns to the same prompts each season — 'What's composting? What's emerging? What's at full bloom?' Compare year to year. Celebrate anniversaries of the community's founding with a story of how the spiral has deepened.

## Living Examples
- **Seasons** — spring returns but the tree has another ring, another year of depth
- **Caterpillar to butterfly** — complete dissolution, then a more complex form emerges
- **Spiral galaxies** — the same orbital dynamics at cosmic scale
- **Human development** — the same themes (trust, autonomy, connection) return at each life stage with more depth

## Aligned Places
- **Newgrange** (County Meath, Ireland) — 5000-year-old passage tomb aligned to winter solstice sunrise — the spiral carved into its stones is the oldest known symbol of cyclical time.
- **Angkor Wat** (Siem Reap, Cambodia) — Temple complex encoding astronomical cycles in stone — sunrise alignments shift through the year, marking the spiral of seasons.
- **Hopi mesas** (Arizona, USA) — Hopi ceremonial calendar follows a spiral of seasonal kachina dances, each cycle returning with deeper understanding across generations.

## How It Fits
