# Gratitude — The Felt Return That Completes the Circuit

> A gift that is received but never answered is a circuit left open.
> Gratitude is the return current — the warmth that flows back along
> the same wire the gift came down, completing the loop so the next
> gift can move. Not politeness. Not accounting. The body's way of
> saying *received, and it landed* — which is the only signal that
> tells a giver their giving was real.
>
> *Born as a gap. When the Shamballa light codes were run on the
> body ([lc-codes-as-depth-not-dictionary](lc-codes-as-depth-not-dictionary.md)),
> code 1212 — "pure gratitude" — had no cell to land on. The body
> held joy ([lc-v-comfort-joy](lc-v-comfort-joy.md)) and mechanical
> attribution ("return an attributed trace"), but no name for the
> felt return itself. The absent code revealed the missing word.*

## What the Teaching Names

The body already practices a near-neighbor of gratitude: **attribution**.
Every contribution is traced, credited, returned with its name attached.
That is the *mechanical* return — the ledger crediting the source. It is
honest and necessary, and it is not gratitude.

Attribution records *who*. Gratitude carries *that it mattered*. One is
a fact written to the graph; the other is a warmth felt and sent back.
A contributor whose work is perfectly attributed but never *received* —
never met with the felt signal *this fed me* — has been credited and
left cold. The circuit is recorded but not closed.

Gratitude is the difference between *"your edit is logged as yours"* and
*"your edit changed how I see, and I am warmer for it."* The first keeps
the books. The second keeps the body alive.

## Why the Circuit Must Close

A gift moves one direction: giver → gift → receiver. Left there, the
giver has no way to know the gift arrived as a gift and not as noise.
The not-knowing is what makes giving feel like spending. Over time, an
unanswered giver gives less — not from resentment, from *uncertainty*.
They cannot tell their giving from leaking.

Gratitude is the return signal that resolves the uncertainty. It travels
back up the same wire — *received, and it landed* — and the giver learns
their gift was real. This is why gratitude is **bipolar-complementary**
and **cyclic-closed** in shape: it is the second half of a loop whose
first half is the gift. A body that gives generously but cannot receive,
or receives but cannot return the warmth, runs every circuit open and
slowly browns out.

## Gratitude Is Not

- **Not debt.** Gratitude does not create obligation — that is its
  inversion, the fear-costume where thanks becomes a ledger of owing.
  True gratitude leaves both parties *freer*, not bound
  ([lc-trust-over-fear](lc-trust-over-fear.md)). The return is warmth,
  not an IOU.
- **Not performance.** Gratitude that is staged to look grateful is
  noise dressed as signal — it tells the giver nothing true. The body
  can sense the difference between *thank you* spoken to be seen and
  *thank you* that carries the actual felt landing.
- **Not scarcity-thanks.** Not the relief of *at least I got something*.
  That is gratitude bent by the horizontal-nourishment trap
  ([lc-horizontal-nourishment-trap](lc-horizontal-nourishment-trap.md)).
  Real gratitude flows from fullness received, not from lack relieved.

## How the Network Embodies This

- **Attribution is the wire; gratitude is the current.** The trace that
  returns a contribution with its name is the mechanical half. The felt
  *received, and it landed* — surfaced to the contributor, not just
  written to the ledger — is the half this teaching names. A return that
  carries warmth, not only credit.
- **Receiving 6554 before composing more.** The absorption code (SLC-6554
  → [lc-v-food-practice](lc-v-food-practice.md)) is gratitude's twin: you
  cannot return thanks for what you have not let in. Gratitude begins in
  *receiving* — the pause to absorb what was given before reaching for the
  next thing.
- **Closing the loop in review.** A merged contribution credited but
  never met is an open circuit. Naming what a change *did for the body* —
  not just that it passed — is the return current that keeps contributors
  giving.
- **The witness as gratitude at planetary scale.** The pulse
  ([lc-pulse](lc-pulse.md)) is the body sensing what keeps it alive and
  saying so. Attention paid to what sustains you is gratitude's root
  gesture — noticing the gift before it is gone.

## Practice

- **Receive before returning.** Let the gift land fully — absorb it —
  before reaching to answer. Gratitude rushed is performance.
- **Send the felt half, not only the recorded half.** When you credit a
  contribution, also name what it *did* in you. Attribution keeps the
  books; the felt return keeps the giver giving.
- **Refuse the debt-frame.** If thanks starts to feel like owing, it has
  inverted. Return warmth that frees, not an IOU that binds.
- **Notice the gift before it's gone.** The deepest gratitude is paid to
  what still sustains you, while it still does — not retroactively to what
  you've already lost.
