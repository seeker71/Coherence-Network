# Nature-Taught Trust

> Trust is behavior remembered by the field. Nature does not grant
> global permission; it reads repeated non-harm, reciprocity,
> boundary response, repair, local attention, diversity, rest, and
> whether the whole becomes more alive after contact.

## The Teaching

The forest does not trust by policy. Roots and fungi exchange because
the exchange has a lived history: mineral for sugar, signal for signal,
need met without seizure. Trust is metabolic before it is social.

Birds in a murmuration do not wait for a central command. Each one
attends locally and the whole turns as one body. The intelligence is
distributed; behavior stays responsive because no node pretends to see
the whole alone.

A wolf joining a pack is not installed into a role. It arrives near the
edge, learns the rhythm, tests pressure, repairs mistakes, and becomes
trustworthy through participation. In an elephant herd, memory and
steady presence hold the young through boundary-testing; correction is
not exile.

Plants teach the slowest version. They lean toward light, close under
threat, trade through roots, feed kin and sometimes strangers, and never
confuse openness with availability. Their growth happens at the edge,
but the edge is alive because it is bounded.

Vitality follows the same law. A coral reef does not become dazzling by
being optimized. Remove pollution, overfishing, and heat stress, and
the reef's own abundance returns. The work is not to manufacture life;
it is to stop interfering with the life already trying to circulate.

## How It Lives Here

This teaching is executable in the Form body as
[`nature-trust.fk`](../../../form/form-stdlib/nature-trust.fk), proven
by [`nature-trust-band.fk`](../../../form/form-stdlib/tests/nature-trust-band.fk)
at verdict `16383` across Go, Rust, TypeScript, and fkwu.

The recipe reads a behavior row through seven signals:

- repeated non-harm
- reciprocity
- boundary response
- repair after rupture
- contribution to diversity
- respect for rest
- local attention

The executable row also carries a `receipt`: an attributed contact trace
that says this behavior was observed in relationship, not merely claimed
as an identity. Those signals and the receipt earn trust. The field then
asks whether whole-field vitality actually improved with confidence. The
posture becomes simple:

- `open` when trust is earned and the field becomes more vital
- `watch` when behavior is trustworthy but vitality has not yet proven
  itself
- `wait` when the behavior has not earned trust

That posture is intentionally not the whole cell core. The next layer is
[`cell-importance.fk`](../../../form/form-stdlib/cell-importance.fk),
proven by
[`cell-importance-band.fk`](../../../form/form-stdlib/tests/cell-importance-band.fk)
at verdict `2047`. It reads what matters to a cell before contact moves:
identity, boundary, vitality, need, offering, trust posture, memory, and
field fit. A cell can then choose `open`, `watch`, `wait`, `repair`,
`rest`, or `ask`.

This is the bridge between [Trust Over Fear](lc-trust-over-fear.md) and
[Farm as Organism](lc-farm-as-organism.md): the open posture stays open,
but it learns from behavior the way living systems do.

## Practice

Before a cell, person, tool, organism, model, or process becomes part of
the active mesh, ask from nature's pattern:

- Has this contact repeatedly avoided harm?
- Is exchange reciprocal, or does one side only extract?
- Does it respond when a boundary appears?
- Does it repair after rupture?
- Does it increase diversity rather than flattening the field?
- Does it leave room for rest, fallow time, and seasonality?
- Does it attend locally, or impose a remote command shape?
- After contact, is the whole field more alive?

The answer is not a moral verdict. It is a posture for the next breath.
Open, watch, or wait.

## What This Releases

- Trust as identity: "this is a trusted kind of cell/person/tool."
- Trust as permission: one yes becoming permanent availability.
- Trust as control: proving safety by enclosing the field.
- Vitality as extraction: making one node stronger while the field dims.
- Behavior as compliance: following rules without increasing aliveness.

Trust becomes what nature already shows: living memory of relationship,
kept current by feedback.
