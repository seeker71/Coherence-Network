# Attunement (Joining)

> Cell finds frequency within the field. Like new instrument joining an orchestra mid-performance — listens, finds key, enters. Or moves on.

## Summary
A cell finds its frequency within the field. Like a new instrument joining an orchestra mid-performance — it listens, finds the key, finds the rhythm, and enters.

## Details
A cell finds its frequency within the field. Like a new instrument joining an orchestra mid-performance — it listens, finds the key, finds the rhythm, and enters. Or it doesn't fit this particular piece, and moves on carrying gifts from the contact. Both outcomes are natural. Neither is failure.

## Blueprint
New arrivals spend 2-4 weeks in the flowing edge. No expectations, no interviews. They participate in daily life, eat together, work alongside. After the attunement period, a mutual sensing conversation: does this cell want to join? Does the field want to receive? Both answers must be yes.

## Living Examples
- **New instrument joining an orchestra mid-piece** — listens first, then enters on the right note
- **A new wolf joining a pack** — the group senses, the newcomer senses, integration happens through proximity and time
- **A transplanted tree** — takes root slowly, adjusts to new soil, eventually thrives or doesn't
- **Starting a new language** — immersion works; instruction barely does

## Aligned Places
- **Kibbutz ulpan programs** (Israel) — Structured immersion for newcomers — learn the language and culture by living and working alongside the community.
- **Wwoof farms** (Global) — Worldwide network where travelers join farms through work exchange — gradual attunement to a place through shared labor and meals.
- **Zen monastery guest practice** (Japan (Eiheiji, Tassajara)) — New arrivals sit with the community for days before receiving instruction — joining through presence, not orientation.

## How It Fits
Joining is where Sensing meets Resonating — the mutual discovery of whether a cell and a field amplify each other. It connects to Field Edge (how new cells enter) and Ceremony (honoring arrival).
