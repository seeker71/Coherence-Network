## Summary

## Details
Always learning through immersion. The way a wolf pup learns to hunt by being with the pack — no instruction, just participation in what's alive. Play is the primary discovery mode. The field's collective intelligence is accessible through relationship, not through courses. Mastery radiates naturally from cells that have developed depth.

## Blueprint
No curriculum. Apprenticeship model: learn by doing alongside someone experienced. Community 'skill shares' monthly — anyone can teach anything. Children learn by participating in real adult activities, not simulated ones. Library of tools, seeds, books, and instruments open to all.

## Living Examples
- **Wolf pup learning to hunt** — by being with the pack, watching, playing, gradually participating
- **Apprenticeship tradition** — learning by doing alongside someone further along
- **Children in hunter-gatherer societies** — learn everything through free play and observation, no formal schooling
- **Mycelial network** — the entire forest's knowledge of soil chemistry, water flow, pest threats is distributed through the network

## Aligned Places
- **Reggio Emilia preschools** (Emilia-Romagna, Italy) — Child-led learning where the environment is the third teacher — discovery through immersion, not instruction.
- **Schumacher College** (Devon, England) — Learning through living together — ecology, economics, and arts taught through hands-on practice and community life.
- **Swaraj University** (Udaipur, Rajasthan, India) — Self-designed learning journeys — no curriculum, no exams, discovery through apprenticeship and community engagement.

## How It Fits
Discovery is the Expressing flow as learning. It connects to Play (play IS discovery), Offering (mastery shared IS teaching), and Field Intelligence (collective knowing).
