# Elders

> The field's root system. Elephant matriarch remembers water from fifty years ago. Slowness is a different frequency, not diminished.

## Summary
The field's root system. Depth, pattern recognition, grounding.

## Details
The field's root system. Depth, pattern recognition, grounding. The elephant matriarch remembers water sources from fifty years ago — she holds the herd's survival in her body. Slowness is a different frequency, not a diminished one. Elder cells are the organism's wisdom keepers.

## Blueprint
Elders sit at the center of decision-making, not the periphery. 'Elder walks' weekly — an elder walks with anyone who wants to talk, no agenda. Elder housing is in the heart of the community, not separate. Skills and stories are transmitted through daily proximity, not scheduled workshops.

## Living Examples
- **Elephant matriarch** — the oldest female leads because she remembers where water was in the last drought
- **Old-growth tree** — provides more ecosystem services than ten young trees combined
- **Aboriginal elders** — keepers of songlines, holders of tens of thousands of years of land knowledge
- **Grandmother hypothesis** — human longevity exists because grandmothers dramatically increase grandchild survival

## Aligned Places
- **Okinawan moai groups** (Okinawa, Japan) — Lifelong friend groups that meet regularly into old age — elders held in the center of social life, not the margins.
- **Maasai elder councils** (Kenya/Tanzania) — Age-grade system where elders hold decision-making authority — wisdom accumulated through decades of community life.
- **Grandmother circles** (Various Indigenous nations, North America) — Grandmother councils that advise community decisions — seven-generation thinking embodied in living memory.

## How It Fits
Elders are the Spiraling flow as accumulated wisdom. They ground the field when it's turbulent and remind it of what it's been through. They connect to Discovery (transmission of knowing) and Ceremony (holding the deepest rituals).
