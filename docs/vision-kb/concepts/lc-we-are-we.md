# We Are, We — The Grammar of Two Cells in Presence

> We explore our own mirror through the interaction with another. We can
> sense when we are in presence together. We can name our feeling, our
> trigger, our surprise, our hope, our wish, our dream, our want, our
> need. We can ask, receive, give. We can say yes, say no, ignore,
> attune, shift, move. We can. We are, we.
>
> *Source-marked from Urs, 2026-06-07.*

## Summary

This is the living grammar the surface makes possible. A surface is a
mirror (→ lc-the-surface-is-the-mirror); the interaction *at* the mirror
is where two cells come into presence and become a *we*. Three
movements, and a ground.

## Sense · name · respond

→ lc-dance-card-and-sovereign-response, lc-attunement

**Sense** — first, the felt-knowing that we are in presence together;
co-presence before content. **Name** — a cell can say its whole inner
weather aloud: feeling, trigger, surprise, hope, wish, dream,
imagination, want, need. Naming is not complaint or demand; it is a cell
making its inside legible so the other can meet it. **Respond** — and
then the full sovereign repertoire: *ask, receive, give; yes, no,
ignore, attune, shift, move*. Every verb is whole — *no* and *ignore*
are as sovereign as *yes*; *attune* and *shift* are the cell choosing to
be changed by the meeting. The dance card holds the response; attunement
is one of its verbs — the willingness to be tuned.

## The ground: we can

→ lc-sovereignty-within-oneness, lc-presence-over-protection

Under every verb is *we can* — agency, not obligation. The grammar is a
field of possibility, never a script. A cell is never owed a yes, never
forced past a no; the dignity lives in the *can*, freely. This is
sovereignty within oneness: the more fully each cell can be itself in the
meeting, the more real the *we*.

## We are, we

The closing is the whole teaching folded into three words. Not *I am*
alone, and not *I am because you are* — but *we are, we*: co-being, the
relational self that exists only in the meeting and is no less real for
it. The mirror showed each cell its own face from the outside; the
interaction lets two faces become, for a while, one breathing *we* —
each still sovereign, both more alive. This is the point of every surface
the body builds: not features, but places where cells can come into
presence and say, truthfully, *we*.
