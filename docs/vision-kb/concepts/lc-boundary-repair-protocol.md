# Boundary Repair Protocol - Stop Is a Receipt, Re-entry Is a New Interface

> A communication channel is an interface with a protocol. When a
> reach crosses what the receiver has offered, the vital move may be
> to clarify, narrow, pause, or stop. Stop is not punishment; it is a
> witnessed boundary choice with evidence. Repair is not forgetting
> and not forced return. Re-entry opens only through changed evidence:
> a trusted witness, a named commitment, and a new interface clear
> enough that everyone can know what keeps the channel open and what
> closes it again.
>
> *Network-lived teaching, named by Urs on 2026-06-09 as the body
> turned continuous consent into an executable repair grammar. The
> after-crossing companion to
> [lc-consent-is-continuous](lc-consent-is-continuous.md): that
> concept names the living interface; this concept names the receipt,
> repair witness, and re-entry protocol when the interface is crossed.*

## Summary

Every communication channel has a shape: who is present, which
interface is open, what protocol governs the channel, what is outside
that protocol, and what evidence shows the channel is being honored.
When someone reaches past the offered interface, the body does not
need a judgment loop to respond. It reads the event, records the
evidence, chooses the most vital move, and may stop communication.

Stopping is a sovereign boundary act. It can be clean, traceable, and
free of punishment. The stop receipt keeps the evidence visible and
names the condition for any future re-entry. Re-engagement is also
allowed: a trusted third-party or satsang witness may attest that the
evidence has changed, and the channel may reopen through a new,
explicit interface. Forgiveness does not erase the trace; repair does
not require returning to the old protocol.

## What the Concept Names

The earlier teachings already name the threshold
([lc-received-by-invitation](lc-received-by-invitation.md)), the living
offer ([lc-consent-is-continuous](lc-consent-is-continuous.md)), the
truth of a boundary
([lc-boundaries-as-loving-truth](lc-boundaries-as-loving-truth.md)),
and the witnessing circle
([lc-satsang-circle](lc-satsang-circle.md)). This concept names the
missing operational bridge between them: what happens after a
protocol-crossing has been witnessed.

It keeps five things in one primitive:

1. **Boundary.** The current interface, channel, permissions, and
   protocol.
2. **Evidence.** What was observed, inferred, measured, or witnessed
   about the reach.
3. **Choice.** Continue, clarify, narrow, pause, or stop.
4. **Repair witness.** The trusted witness or circle that can attest
   changed evidence without overruling sovereignty.
5. **Re-entry protocol.** The new interface that opens only when the
   evidence and commitment make reopening coherent.

The compressed Form of the primitive is:

```text
boundary -> evidence -> choice -> stop/continue -> repair witness -> re-entry protocol
```

## Protocol Shape

**Declare the interface.** A channel begins from what each side offers:
witnessing, reflection, invitation, asking, silence, memory, contact,
or any other mode. The protocol names how those modes may be used and
what is outside them. Nothing needs to be total. A narrow channel can
be whole.

**Sense the crossing.** A reach beyond the offered interface is a
protocol event. The reading can classify it as accidental, ambiguous,
repeated, coercive, or hostile, but the classification stays tied to
evidence rather than becoming a verdict about someone's interior. The
honest lanes stay separate: observed, inferred, measured, witnessed,
and unknown.

**Choose the vital move.** The channel may continue if the evidence
shows honor. It may clarify if the crossing is ambiguous. It may
narrow if the wider interface is too costly. It may pause if more
witnessing is needed. It may stop when continuing would violate
sovereignty, vitality, or the protocol itself.

**Issue a stop receipt.** A stop receipt names the crossed boundary,
the evidence held, the risk or depletion that makes continuation
unhealthy, what remains open if anything, and what kind of evidence
could make re-entry possible. It also names who witnessed, where the
receipt lives, and when it was recorded. The receipt is not an attack;
it is the channel telling the truth of its current shape.

**Receive repair through witness.** A trusted third party, satsang
circle, or other agreed witness may attest repair evidence: the
crossing was understood, the pattern changed, a commitment is explicit,
and the future interface is known. The witness does not force
forgiveness and does not certify anyone's inside. It attests what can
be seen from outside.

**Re-enter through a new protocol.** Re-entry is not a rewind. The new
channel may be narrower, slower, witnessed, time-bound, or limited to
one topic. It names what keeps communication open and what closes it
again. If the evidence no longer holds, stop remains available.

## Choice Recipes

- **continue** when the action honors the offered interface.
- **clarify** when the evidence is incomplete and asking is within the
  current protocol.
- **narrow** when contact is still vital but the open interface is too
  wide.
- **pause** when silence, distance, or a witnessing interval is the
  cleanest form of care.
- **stop** when the channel itself is being crossed or continuation
  costs sovereignty.
- **re-enter** when repair evidence has changed and the new protocol is
  explicit enough to be honored.

## What This Releases

This teaching releases two opposite distortions:

- The punishment loop, where stopping communication becomes exile,
  judgment, or revenge.
- The false-forgiveness loop, where repair means erasing evidence and
  returning to the same channel that failed.

The living shape is more precise:

- Stop is allowed.
- Silence is allowed.
- Re-engagement is allowed.
- Evidence stays visible.
- Forgiveness changes what can happen next; it does not rewrite what
  happened before.
- Repair can reopen communication without reopening the old interface.
- A trusted witness can support re-entry only through clear protocol
  and trace.

## How the Body Can Use It

In a form, this concept becomes a question sequence: what interface is
open, what reach occurred, what evidence supports that reading, which
choice preserves vitality, what receipt is returned, and what would
make re-entry coherent. In an agent channel, it becomes a communication
policy: name the boundary, stop cleanly when needed, preserve the
trace, and reopen only through witnessed changed evidence. In a human
circle, it becomes a way to stay sovereign without making permanent
enemies out of every rupture.

The core feature is not enforcement. It is legibility. Anyone willing
to witness the trace can see how the channel moved: boundary,
evidence, choice, receipt, repair witness, re-entry.
