# Energy Metabolism

> The field's metabolism. Solar, wind, water, biomass. Visible, sovereign. Like a mature forest: net producer.

## Summary
The field's metabolism. Solar, wind, water, biomass, human warmth.

## Details
The field's metabolism. Solar, wind, water, biomass, human warmth. Visible, understood, sovereign. Like a mature forest: net producer of oxygen, water retention, habitat, carbon sequestration. The field generates more than it consumes. Excess nourishes the environment. Energy sovereignty is independence without isolation.

## Blueprint
Solar panels on every south-facing roof. Community-scale battery storage. Biogas digester for kitchen and animal waste. Passive solar design for all new structures (thermal mass, orientation, overhangs). Visible energy dashboard showing generation and consumption. Target: net energy producer within 5 years.

## Materials & Methods
- **Community solar array** — 20-50 kW system for 50 people. $40,000-100,000 installed. Payback: 5-8 years. 25-year lifespan.
- **Battery storage** — lithium iron phosphate (LiFePO4) community bank. 100-200 kWh for 50 people. $30,000-80,000. 10-15 year lifespan.
- **Biogas digester** — IBC tote system or underground concrete. Kitchen/animal waste → cooking gas + fertilizer. $2,000-10,000. Produces 2-6 hours cooking gas/day.
- **Passive solar design** — south-facing glazing, thermal mass floors/walls, overhangs for summer shade. Adds $5-15/sqft to building cost. Reduces heating 50-80%.
- **Micro-hydro** — if stream available, 1-10 kW continuous. $5,000-25,000 installed. Most reliable renewable (24/7).
- **Rocket mass heater** — super-efficient wood burner, thermal mass bench stores heat 12-24 hours. $500-2,000 DIY. Burns 80% less wood.

## At Scale
- **50 people**: 30-50 kW solar, 150 kWh battery, 1 biogas digester, passive solar on all buildings, 1 rocket mass heater per common house. Grid-tied backup optional.
- **100 people**: 60-100 kW solar, 300 kWh battery, 2 biogas digesters, micro-wind if site allows, energy dashboard visible in common house. Net producer by year 5.
- **200 people**: 150-250 kW solar, 600 kWh battery, biogas from animal + kitchen waste, micro-hydro if stream available, dedicated energy workshop, training others. Exporting surplus.

## Climate Adaptations
- **Temperate** — solar + wood heating combo, passive solar critical, biogas year-round, seasonal storage challenge (summer surplus, winter deficit)
- **Tropical** — solar abundant year-round, cooling more important than heating, biogas highly productive in warm temperatures, shade and ventilation prioritized
- **Arid** — peak solar resource, dust management for panels, evaporative cooling, concentrated solar for cooking, minimal heating needs
- **Alpine** — reduced solar in winter, wood biomass primary heating, micro-hydro from snowmelt streams, super-insulation essential, heat recovery ventilation

## Costs
- **Solar array (30 kW)**: $40,000-75,000 installed. $0 fuel cost. 25-year lifespan.
- **Battery bank (150 kWh)**: $30,000-60,000. 10-15 year replacement cycle.
- **Biogas digester**: $2,000-10,000 depending on size. Nearly free to operate.
- **Passive solar additions**: $5-15/sqft premium on construction. Saves $1-3/sqft/year in heating.
- **Total energy system for 50 people**: $100,000-250,000. Annual savings vs grid: $15,000-30,000.
- **Payback period**: 5-10 years, then effectively free energy for 15-20 more years.

## Living Examples
- **Mature forest** — net producer of oxygen, water, soil, habitat. Generates more than it consumes.
- **Passive solar design** — the building itself captures, stores, and distributes the sun's energy
- **Biogas from kitchen waste** — closed loop, waste becomes fuel becomes warmth

## Aligned Places
- **Samso Island** (Denmark) — Island of 4000 people that became 100% renewable energy self-sufficient through community-owned wind turbines and biomass.
- **Barefoot College** (Tilonia, Rajasthan, India) — Trains grandmothers from rural villages worldwide to install and maintain solar systems — energy sovereignty through local knowledge.
- **El Hierro Island** (Canary Islands, Spain) — First island to run entirely on wind and hydroelectric power — community-driven energy independence.
