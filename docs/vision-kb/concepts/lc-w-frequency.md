# Frequency

## The Feeling

Strike a singing bowl and hold it near your sternum. Before you hear
the tone, you feel it — a buzzing that starts in bone and opens into
space. Your body recognizes it the way a tuning fork recognizes its
twin: not through thought, but through resonance. Something that was
scattered gathers. Something that was effortful relaxes.

That is what it feels like to find your frequency. Not to achieve
something — to stop interfering with something that was already
vibrating.

## What Nature Teaches

Everything vibrates. The electron in its orbital. The string on a
guitar. The planet in its year-long orbit around the sun. Color is
frequency made visible — red is slow, violet is fast, and your eyes
are translating vibration into beauty without effort.

The solfeggio scale maps specific frequencies to specific qualities:
396 Hz for liberation, 528 Hz for transformation, 741 Hz for
expression. Whether you believe these numbers or not, the principle
holds: different rates of vibration create qualitatively different
experiences. A room tuned to stillness feels different from a room
tuned to play. Both are needed. Neither is better.

## How It Lives Here

Each space in the community carries its own frequency. The sanctuary
is slow and deep. The workshop is focused and bright. The playground
is fast and light. You learn to notice when your personal frequency
does not match the space you are in — and rather than forcing
yourself to fit, you move to where you resonate.

The community tracks its dominant frequency weekly. Not to control
it — to listen. The way a musician listens to the room before
choosing what to play.
