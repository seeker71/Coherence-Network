# Phase Transition

> What some call death or loss. The note changes. The chord reorganizes. The music continues. Transformation, not termination.

## Summary
What some vocabularies call death, ending, or loss. The note changes.

## Details
What some vocabularies call death, ending, or loss. The note changes. The chord reorganizes. The music continues. Transformation, not termination. Ice becomes water becomes steam — the substance doesn't vanish, it changes state.

## Blueprint
Honor every ending as a beginning. When someone leaves, a departure ceremony. When a building is decommissioned, a composting ceremony. When a relationship shifts form, acknowledgment without grief. Keep a community 'transitions journal' recording every phase change — reading it reveals the spiral.

## Living Examples
- **Ice → water → steam** — same substance, different expression, nothing lost
- **Caterpillar → chrysalis → butterfly** — complete dissolution, then a more complex form
- **Sunset** — not the death of day but the transition to night, equally alive

## Aligned Places
- **Varanasi cremation ghats** (Uttar Pradesh, India) — Open-air cremations continuous for 3000+ years — the city itself is a threshold between states of being.
- **Sedlec Ossuary (Bone Church)** (Kutna Hora, Czech Republic) — Chapel decorated with 40,000 human skeletons — phase transition made visible, bones as art, death as material.

## How It Fits
Phase Transition replaces 'death' and 'ending' in the vocabulary — reframing every completion as a state change rather than a loss. Without it, the Living Collective has no language for holding grief, departure, and transformation as natural movements of the spiral.
