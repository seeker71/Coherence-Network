# Space

> Qualities not containers. The field arranges around what's alive. Sanctuaries, hearths, workshops, gardens. Like a beehive — alive, temperature-regulated by the collective.

## Summary
Qualities rather than containers. The field arranges itself around what's alive.

## Details
Qualities rather than containers. The field arranges itself around what's alive. Like a beehive — hexagonal efficiency but alive, temperature-regulated by the collective body. Spaces have names by quality: sanctuary (deep quiet), hearth (warmth and nourishment), nest (rest and intimacy), clearing (open gathering), creation nook (focused making), movement ground (body expression), water temple (cleansing and rejuvenation). No space is assigned to a person. You flow to what serves you.

The Hakka tulou roundhouses of Fujian province show what this looks like at scale: circular communal structures housing 300-800 people for 800+ years, with shared courtyards and individual rooms. Findhorn's ecovillage demonstrates it in a modern context: turf-roofed buildings, whisky-barrel homes, the Universal Hall for community gathering — each structure designed from within for its purpose, not from above for control.

Key design principles: inside-outside as gradient not boundary. Thermal mass (earth, stone, water) for passive temperature. Water visible as it flows. Living roofs for habitat, insulation, beauty. Curved walls (no right angles) for acoustic warmth and structural strength. Materials that age beautifully: cob darkens, timber silvers, stone gathers moss. Every structure should be able to compost back into the land within 100 years.

## Blueprint
Name every space by quality, not function: 'The Hearth' (kitchen-gathering), 'The Nest' (rest), 'The Clearing' (open gathering), 'The Den' (focused creation), 'The Spring' (water and cleansing). Use movable partitions so spaces can reconfigure. Ensure every space has natural light, a plant, and something beautiful.

## Materials & Methods
- **Cob** — clay+sand+straw sculpted by hand. $5-15/sqft materials. Best for curved walls, thermal mass. Requires no forms.
- **Rammed earth** — compressed soil in forms. $15-30/sqft. Best for long walls, temperature regulation. 2000+ year durability.
- **Timber frame** — local wood joinery. $20-40/sqft. Best for large spans, rapid construction. Can be raised communally.
- **Bamboo** — engineered bamboo (Simon Velez method). $8-20/sqft. Best for tropical climates. 3-5 year growth cycle.
- **Living roof** — soil+plants over waterproof membrane. $15-25/sqft addition. Insulation, habitat, beauty.

## At Scale
- **50 people**: 1 common house (2000 sqft hearth + 800 sqft clearing), 1 workshop (600 sqft), 15-20 private nests (200-400 sqft each). Total built: ~12,000 sqft.
- **100 people**: 2 common houses (different qualities — one loud, one quiet), 2 workshops, 1 movement ground, 30-40 nests. Total built: ~28,000 sqft.
- **200 people**: Neighborhood clusters of 30-50 around shared courtyards (tulou pattern). 4 common houses, central clearing for 200+, distributed workshops. Total built: ~60,000 sqft.

## Climate Adaptations
- **Temperate** — thick cob/rammed earth walls (18-24"), passive solar south glazing, woodstove hearths, root cellars
- **Tropical** — open-air bamboo structures, cross-ventilation, raised floors, thatched roofs, shade trees within 10ft
- **Arid** — earth-sheltered or adobe, courtyard (riad) pattern, thermal mass for day-night swing, cistern integration
- **Alpine** — super-insulated timber, small windows north/large south, underground connections between buildings, snow-load roofs

## Costs
- **Common house (hearth + clearing)**: $80,000-250,000 depending on materials. Cob/timber lowest, conventional highest.
- **Private nest (300 sqft)**: $15,000-45,000 owner-built cob/timber. $40,000-80,000 contractor-built.
- **Workshop (600 sqft)**: $20,000-60,000. Can be open-air in warm climates ($5,000-15,000).
- **Site infrastructure** (paths, water, power to buildings): $30,000-100,000 for 50-person site.
- **Total for 50 people**: $500K-1.5M built over 3-5 years with sweat equity reducing 40-60%.

## How It Fits
