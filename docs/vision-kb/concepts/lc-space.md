# Space

> Qualities not containers. The field arranges around what's alive. Sanctuaries, hearths, workshops, gardens. Like a beehive — alive, temperature-regulated by the collective.

## Summary
Qualities rather than containers. The field arranges itself around what's alive.

## Details
Qualities rather than containers. The field arranges itself around what's alive. Like a beehive — hexagonal efficiency but alive, temperature-regulated by the collective body. Spaces have names by quality: sanctuary (deep quiet), hearth (warmth and nourishment), nest (rest and intimacy), clearing (open gathering), creation nook (focused making), movement ground (body expression), water temple (cleansing and rejuvenation). No space is assigned to a person. You flow to what serves you.

The Hakka tulou roundhouses of Fujian province show what this looks like at scale: circular communal structures housing 300-800 people for 800+ years, with shared courtyards and individual rooms. Findhorn's ecovillage demonstrates it in a modern context: turf-roofed buildings, whisky-barrel homes, the Universal Hall for community gathering — each structure designed from within for its purpose, not from above for control.

Key design principles: inside-outside as gradient not boundary. Thermal mass (earth, stone, water) for passive temperature. Water visible as it flows. Living roofs for habitat, insulation, beauty. Curved walls (no right angles) for acoustic warmth and structural strength. Materials that age beautifully: cob darkens, timber silvers, stone gathers moss. Every structure should be able to compost back into the land within 100 years.

## Blueprint
Name every space by quality, not function: 'The Hearth' (kitchen-gathering), 'The Nest' (rest), 'The Clearing' (open gathering), 'The Den' (focused creation), 'The Spring' (water and cleansing). Use movable partitions so spaces can reconfigure. Ensure every space has natural light, a plant, and something beautiful.

## Living Examples
- **Beehive** — hexagonal, thermally regulated, continuously rebuilt, alive
- **Japanese tea room** — minimal, intentional, every element serves presence
- **The forest itself** — clearings for sun, canopy for shade, edges for diversity
- **Moroccan riad** — inward-facing courtyard, water, garden, sky visible from every room

## Aligned Places
- **Hakka tulou (roundhouses)** (Fujian, China) — Circular communal houses up to 5 stories, 800+ years old. 300-800 people living in one structure. Shared courtyard, individual rooms, collective life.
- **Solarpunk co-housing** (Various (emerging)) — Modern co-housing designed with shared spaces, gardens, kitchens. Private nests + communal life.

## Aligned Communities
- [Findhorn](https://www.ecovillagefindhorn.com/) — Turf-roofed buildings, whisky-barrel homes, Universal Hall. Lowest ecological footprint measured.
- [Hakka Tulou](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fujian_tulou) — 800-year-old circular communal houses. 300-800 people per structure. Shared courtyard, individual rooms.
- [Earthship Biotecture](https://earthship.com/) — Buildings that ARE infrastructure: passive solar, water harvesting, food growing, waste treatment integrated.
- [Arcosanti](https://arcosanti.org/) — Paolo Soleri's arcology — compact, mixed-use, architecture + ecology. 50 years of building a bridge city.

## How It Fits
Space is the Through Body expression of Nourishing. Every other expression needs space to happen in — the hearth for Nourishment, the sanctuary for Stillness, the clearing for Ceremony.
