# Ceremony

## Summary

## Details
Honoring what wants honoring. Like elephants circling a dying member — touching, breathing together — not ritualized, just the field's natural response. Fire circles, water rituals, dawn singing, seasonal spirals. The form follows the moment.

Damanhur in Italy has carved underground Temples of Humankind over 40 years — rooms of extraordinary beauty hidden beneath a hillside, covered in floor-to-ceiling sacred art, stained glass, mosaics. The temples aren't religious — they're an expression of what human creativity can achieve when art IS the foundation of community life. Every Damanhurian contributes to the temples' ongoing creation.

The Burning Man temple shows another frequency: a temporary structure of breathtaking beauty, built by hundreds of volunteers, filled with the hopes and sorrows of 70,000 people, then burned in collective silence. The impermanence IS the ceremony.

In indigenous traditions worldwide, ceremony is woven into daily life: the Aboriginal corroboree, the Lakota sweat lodge, the Andean despacho, the West African libation. None of these are performances — they're the community in direct conversation with the living world.

Our ceremonies emerge from presence, not prescription. But they carry echoes of all these traditions: fire as gathering point, water as purification, earth as grounding, sky as expansion, breath as shared rhythm, voice as shared vibration.

## Blueprint
Fire circle every new moon. Seasonal ceremonies at solstice and equinox. Arrival ceremony for new members (simple: the community sings them in). Departure ceremony (the community sings them out). No prescribed forms — let the moment teach the ceremony.

## Living Examples
- **Elephants mourning** — the herd circles, touches, stands in silence. Returns to bones years later.
- **Japanese tea ceremony** — every movement intentional, beauty in simplicity, presence distilled
- **Cacao ceremony (Mesoamerican)** — heart-opening plant shared in circle, setting intention
- **Solstice bonfire** — the oldest human ceremony, marking the turning point of light and dark

## Aligned Places
- **Lakota Sun Dance grounds** (Pine Ridge, South Dakota, USA) — Annual ceremony of sacrifice, prayer, and renewal — the community gathers around the sacred tree for days of fasting and dancing.
- **Balinese temple ceremonies** (Bali, Indonesia) — Daily offerings (canang sari) woven into every aspect of life — ceremony is not separate from living, it IS living.
- **Dia de los Muertos celebrations** (Oaxaca, Mexico) — Community-wide honoring of the dead with altars, marigolds, food, music — death held as part of the living field.

## Aligned Communities
- [Damanhur Temples](https://thetemples.org/) — Underground Temples of Humankind — 40 years of sacred art carved beneath a hillside. Art as community foundation.
- [Burning Man Temple](https://burningman.org/) — Temporary sacred structure, community-built, filled with intention, burned in collective silence.
- [Taizé Community](https://www.taize.fr/) — Ecumenical community in France. Simple repetitive chanting that attunes 100,000+ visitors per year.
