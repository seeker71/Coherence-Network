# Nourishment

> The field builds itself from its environment. Kitchen-hearth is the center. Meals are resonance practice. Like campfire that shaped every tribe.

## Summary

## Details
The field builds itself from its environment. The kitchen-hearth is the center — like the campfire that shaped every human tribe for 400,000 years. Meals are resonance practice: cells aligning through shared substance. What the field grows and eats expresses its relationship with its land.

The 7-layer food forest is the foundation: canopy (nut trees — walnut, chestnut, hazelnut), understory (fruit — apple, pear, plum, cherry, fig), shrub layer (berries — blackberry, currant, gooseberry), herbaceous (herbs, greens, medicinal plants), ground cover (strawberries, clover, nasturtium), root layer (Jerusalem artichoke, sweet potato, garlic), vine layer (grape, kiwi, passion fruit on every vertical surface). Within 3-7 years, the food forest produces with minimal input.

The fermentation corner transforms raw food into living culture: sauerkraut, kimchi, miso, kombucha, kefir, sourdough, vinegar. Each jar is a collaboration with microorganisms — fermentation IS community at the microbial scale. Sandor Katz's work shows how every traditional culture developed its own ferments: the Korean kimchi pot, the Japanese miso barrel, the German sauerkraut crock.

Meals follow the land's rhythm: in spring, the first tender greens and asparagus. Summer's explosion of tomatoes, peppers, squash, stone fruit. Autumn's root vegetables, apples, preservation season. Winter's stored abundance — dried, smoked, fermented, canned. The kitchen never serves out of season. You eat what the land offers THIS week.

## Blueprint
Build a 7-layer food forest within the first year. Start a fermentation corner (sauerkraut, kimchi, kombucha, sourdough). Communal meals follow the land's seasons — menus change weekly based on what's ripe. Children participate in every stage: planting, harvesting, cooking, composting.

## Living Examples
- **The campfire** — 400,000 years of human gathering, cooking, storytelling around shared flame
- **Korean communal dining (banchan)** — many small shared dishes, everyone eating from the same table
- **Garden-to-table flow** — picking food and eating it within the hour, maximum frequency
- **Bread making** — transforming grain through fermentation, water, heat, time, presence

## Aligned Places
- **Satoyama landscapes** (Japan) — Centuries-old forest-garden-rice paddy mosaics where human cultivation increased biodiversity — food as relationship with land.
- **Oaxacan milpa farming** (Oaxaca, Mexico) — Three Sisters polyculture (corn, beans, squash) practiced for 7000+ years — the original food forest at human scale.
- **Kerala toddy shops and rice boats** (Kerala, India) — Communal eating culture where meals are served on banana leaves, everyone eats together, and food is medicine.

## Aligned Communities
- [Zaytuna Farm](https://www.zaytunafarm.com/) — Geoff Lawton's 66-acre permaculture food forest demonstration. The gold standard for food forest design.
- [Auroville Farm Group](https://auroville.org/) — Organic farming, seed saving, and food forests in tropical India. 50+ years of collective food growing.
- [Polyface Farm](https://www.polyfacefarms.com/) — Joel Salatin's regenerative multi-species rotation. Soil building through animal integration.

## How It Fits
Nourishment is where the Nourishing flow becomes tangible. The kitchen-hearth is the field's literal heart — where raw environment becomes shared vitality.
