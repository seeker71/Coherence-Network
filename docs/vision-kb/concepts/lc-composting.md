# Composting

## Summary
How the field transforms what has completed into nourishment for what's emerging. A leaf falls, becomes soil, feeds the tree, grows new leaves.

## Details
How the field transforms what has completed into nourishment for what's emerging. A leaf falls, becomes soil, feeds the tree, grows new leaves. Nothing is lost — recycled at a higher octave. Not ending but transformation. Not loss but phase transition. The field holds this process with the same presence it holds everything.

## Blueprint
Physical composting stations visible throughout the community — food scraps, humanure, garden waste. When a project, practice, or relationship completes, hold a 'composting ceremony': acknowledge what was, harvest the learnings, release the form. Annual review: what structures have completed their lifecycle?

## Living Examples
- **Forest floor** — fallen leaves become soil become new trees in continuous cycle
- **Fermentation** — 'spoiled' food becomes kimchi, wine, cheese, miso — more complex, more nourishing
- **Composting toilet** — human waste becomes soil that feeds gardens that feed humans
- **Phoenix myth across cultures** — the fire IS the rebirth, destruction IS creation

## Aligned Places
- **Natural burial grounds** (Various (growing movement)) — Bodies returned to earth without embalming. The body becomes soil. Phase transition made tangible.

## How It Fits
Composting replaces the concept of grief, loss, death. Nothing ends — everything transforms. It connects to Phase Transitions (the larger lifecycle) and Rest (composting IS integration).
