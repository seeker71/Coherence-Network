# Harmonizing Practice

> How the field tunes itself. Sound, breath, movement, shared stillness. Singing bowls, drums, voice, forest bathing.

## Summary
How the field tunes itself. Morning attunement.

## Details
How the field tunes itself. Morning attunement. Sound. Breath. Movement. Shared stillness. What are the instruments? Singing bowls, drums, voice, silence, dance, immersion in nature. What does a pod of humans tuning together feel like? The practices from indigenous sound healing, kirtan, overtone singing, forest bathing all point toward the same thing: cells attuning to each other and to the field through shared vibration.

## Blueprint
Daily: morning circle (5-15 min, sensing round, optional sound). Weekly: longer harmonizing session (singing, movement, silence). Seasonal: full-day attunement at each solstice/equinox. Instruments: singing bowls, drums, voice, didgeridoo, silence.

## Living Examples
- **Kirtan (devotional chanting)** — call and response, the group finds one voice through many
- **Overtone singing (Tuvan)** — one voice producing multiple simultaneous frequencies
- **Forest bathing (shinrin-yoku)** — attuning to the forest's frequency through slow, sensory immersion
- **Group breathwork (holotropic, Wim Hof)** — shared breathing rhythm alters collective state

## Aligned Places
- **Taizé Community** (Burgundy, France) — Ecumenical community. Simple repetitive chanting that 100,000 visitors per year find profoundly attuning.
- **Findhorn's Universal Hall** (Scotland) — Community-built performance and meditation space. Group meditations, sound healing, collective attunement.

## How It Fits
Harmonizing Practice is the field's daily tuning instrument — without it, cells drift out of resonance and the collective sensing degrades. It connects Sensing (awareness of state) with Stillness (shared presence) into an active, repeatable practice.
