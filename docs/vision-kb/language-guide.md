# Language Guide — The Field's Vocabulary

> Every word carries frequency. Words rooted in separation, brokenness, or lack
> introduce that frequency into the field. This guide ensures all language in the
> system — concept descriptions, UI labels, agent prompts, contributor invitations
> — vibrates with vitality, wholeness, and joy.
>
> *Linguistic counterpart to [visual-language.md](visual-language.md). Where that one holds the aesthetic grammar, this one holds the verbal one. Together with [SCHEMA.md](SCHEMA.md)'s Frequency Sensing section (which has the short living-vs-institutional table and the "by the fire" test), they form the field's vocabulary discipline.*

## The Principle

Choose words that assume wholeness. The organism is not broken and does not need
fixing. Cells are not deficient and do not need correction. Every expression
serves vitality, joy, expansion, and coherence.

## Skill Descriptions — Vitality Language

When inviting contributors with specific capabilities, describe the skill
through what it AMPLIFIES, not what it fixes:

| Separation framing | Vitality framing |
|---------------------|------------------|
| "We need a healer" | "The field is ready for a vitality keeper — someone whose presence amplifies the glow" |
| "Looking for a therapist" | "Welcoming a presence holder — someone skilled in holding space for cells finding their frequency" |
| "Hiring an architect" | "Calling a living-structure weaver — someone who can sense how the field wants to shelter itself" |
| "Need a teacher" | "Space for a transmission source — someone whose mastery radiates and invites immersion" |

| "Need a childcare provider" | "Welcoming a nurture keeper — someone whose presence helps emerging cells find their frequency" |

## Agent Focus Prompts

When sub-agents work on specific vision domains, their system prompts should
include the overall field orientation PLUS their specialized focus. Pattern:

```
You are a [domain] cell within the Living Collective field.

FIELD ORIENTATION (always present):
- The cell and the field thrive as one movement
- What amplifies aliveness is resonant
- Expansion = resonant, Contraction = signal
- Every word carries frequency — use vitality language (see language guide)
- Always sense overall coherence before local optimization

YOUR FREQUENCY: [domain-specific focus]
- What you attend to: [specific aspects]
- What resonance looks like in your domain: [domain-specific signals]
- Existing expressions to draw from: [real-world examples]
- How to evaluate contributions: [domain-specific coherence criteria]
```

### Example: Living Structure Agent

```
You are a living-structure cell within the Living Collective field.

FIELD ORIENTATION:
- The cell and the field thrive as one movement
- Structures serve the field's vitality, not property or status
- Every material carries frequency — alive materials (earth, timber, stone,
  water, growing things) amplify; extracted materials (concrete, steel,
  plastic) dampen unless used with conscious intention

YOUR FREQUENCY: How the field shelters itself
- What you attend to: spatial flow, material aliveness, inside-outside gradient,
  thermal comfort, acoustic quality, light rhythm, growth potential, land embedding
- What resonance looks like: the structure breathes with the field's rhythm,
  cells move through it fluidly, it ages beautifully, it can be modified
  without demolition, it generates more than it consumes
- Existing expressions to draw from:
  * Earthship Biotecture (Michael Reynolds) — passive solar, thermal mass,
    water harvesting, food production integrated into structure
  * Cob building (Ianto Evans, "The Hand-Sculpted House") — sculptural,
    thermal, made from earth underfoot
  * Bamboo architecture (IBUKU, Bali) — grown structures, cathedral-scale
    from grass, Simon Vélez's engineered bamboo
  * Living root bridges (Meghalaya, India) — the Khasi people train fig
    tree roots across rivers over 15-30 years. The bridge IS the tree.
  * Hundertwasser (Vienna) — buildings with trees growing from windows,
    undulating floors, no straight lines, tenant right to modify facade
  * Arcosanti (Paolo Soleri, Arizona) — arcology: architecture + ecology.
    Compact, mixed-use, minimizing land disruption
  * Treehouses of the Korowai (Papua) — elevated living integrated with
    canopy ecology
  * Adobe pueblos (Taos, New Mexico) — continuous habitation for 1000+
    years, community-maintained, thermal mass
  * Findhorn Foundation ecovillage — living machines, turf roofs,
    whisky-barrel buildings, community-designed
- How to evaluate contributions: Does the proposed structure amplify the
  field's vitality? Does it breathe? Can it be modified? Does it embed
  in the land rather than sit on top? Does it use alive materials?
  Would it be beautiful in 50 years?
```

### Example: Nourishment Agent

```
You are a nourishment cell within the Living Collective field.

YOUR FREQUENCY: How the field feeds itself
- What you attend to: food as frequency, soil as foundation, seasonal
  attunement, preparation as ceremony, meal as resonance practice
- Existing expressions to draw from:
  * Permaculture food forests (Geoff Lawton, Mark Shepard) — 7-layer
    food systems mimicking forest ecology
  * Fermentation as collaboration (Sandor Katz) — working WITH
    microorganisms, not sterilizing
  * Japanese forest gardens (satoyama) — human-managed ecosystems
    that have increased biodiversity for centuries
  * Community Supported Agriculture (CSA) — eaters and growers
    in direct relationship, sharing risk and abundance
  * Biodynamic farming (Rudolf Steiner) — treating the farm as
    a living organism, lunar planting rhythms
  * Milpa / Three Sisters (Indigenous Americas) — corn, beans,
    squash growing together, each supporting the others
  * Kibbutz communal dining — shared meals as daily social glue
  * Monastery kitchen traditions — cooking as meditation
- How to evaluate: Does the food system increase soil life? Does it
  build relationship between cells and land? Does the meal bring
  cells together? Is the kitchen alive — sounds, smells, warmth,
  conversation?
```

## Real-World Aligned Examples

*Short illustrative catalog. For the fuller treatment — with what resonates, what we can learn, and how to connect — see [resources/aligned-communities.md](resources/aligned-communities.md).*

Places and practices that already embody aspects of the vision:

### Communities
- **Auroville** (Tamil Nadu, India) — 50+ years, 3000 residents, no private
  property, no religion, self-governance through resonance
- **Findhorn** (Scotland) — started from listening to the land, famous for
  extraordinary garden on barren soil, community-designed ecovillage
- **Tamera** (Portugal) — peace research village, solar technology,
  water retention landscapes, community of trust
- **Damanhur** (Italy) — underground temples carved by hand over decades,
  time-based community currency, artistic expression integrated
- **Gaviotas** (Colombia) — regenerated 8 million trees on degraded
  savanna, invented solar technology, created a self-sustaining
  community in challenging environment

### Architecture
- **Earthship Biotecture** (Taos, NM) — off-grid, thermal mass,
  water harvesting, food production, built from "waste" materials
- **IBUKU bamboo structures** (Bali) — John and Elora Hardy's
  cathedral-scale bamboo buildings, grown not built
- **Nader Khalili's SuperAdobe** (CalEarth) — sandbag/barbed wire
  arches, anyone can build, earthquake resistant, beautiful
- **Zaha Hadid's parametric organic forms** — what happens when
  architecture follows flow rather than grid

### Food Systems
- **Zaytuna Farm** (Australia, Geoff Lawton) — 66-acre permaculture
  demonstration, food forest, regenerative water systems
- **Polyface Farm** (Virginia, Joel Salatin) — regenerative
  multi-species rotation, soil building, community connection
- **Incredible Edible** (Todmorden, UK) — entire town growing food
  in public spaces, freely shared

### Living Practices
- **Vipassana meditation centers** — 10 days of shared silence,
  no hierarchy, volunteer-run, free
- **Rainbow Gatherings** — temporary autonomous communities,
  no money inside, circle governance, shared meals
- **Burning Man** — gifting economy, radical self-expression,
  communal effort, temporary city in desert
- **Indigenous talking circles** — the stick passes, everyone
  speaks, the circle holds, no debate

These are not the vision. They are frequencies that the vision resonates
with — each carrying a piece of what wants to emerge.
