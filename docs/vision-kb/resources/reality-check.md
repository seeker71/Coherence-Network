# Reality Check — What Actually Works in Community Building

> Honest assessment based on deep research (April 2026). Communities that succeed take 5-30 years of grinding work, start small, have transparent governance, and build infrastructure before selling memberships. Projects that lead with grand visions, charismatic founders, high fees, and NDAs produce websites, not villages.

## What We Learn From History (500+ Years of Data)

### The 5 Things That Kill Communities

| # | Failure Mode | How Survivors Avoid It |
|---|-------------|----------------------|
| 1 | **Interpersonal conflict without resolution process** (most common killer) | Written conflict resolution procedures BEFORE first conflict. NVC training. Professional mediation available. Regular sensing rounds. |
| 2 | **Founder syndrome / power concentration** | 6-month rotating leadership (Damanhur). Transparent finances. Written constitution that evolves. Regular role rotation. |
| 3 | **Economic single point of failure** | Multiple income streams (manufacturing + agriculture + education + remote work). 6-month emergency reserves. |
| 4 | **Stuck in ideation, never building** (silent majority of failures) | Time-bounded decisions. Clear milestones. Bias toward action. Start with temporary structures. |
| 5 | **Membership screening too loose** | Multi-stage: visit (1-3 weeks) → provisional (3-6 months) → full membership vote. Clear criteria beyond resonance. |

### Governance That Works By Scale

| Scale | Model | Example |
|-------|-------|---------|
| 10-20 | Direct consensus, everyone in the room | Every seed group |
| 50 | Consensus + skilled facilitation + written process | Dancing Rabbit, Earthaven |
| 100 | Sociocracy (consent-based circles) | ~200 communities on FIC directory |
| 200+ | Federation (neighborhood autonomy + village coordination) | Damanhur (30 nucleos, constitution, elected guides) |
| 500+ | Budding at ~150 (Dunbar's number) | Hutterites (500 colonies, 50,000 people, 500+ years) |

### Economic Models That Sustain Communities

| Model | How it works | Example | Scale |
|-------|-------------|---------|-------|
| Full income sharing | All income pooled, members receive all needs | Svanholm (80/20 split), East Wind, Twin Oaks | 50-100 |
| Shared expenses | Individual income, pooled costs ($500-1500/mo/person) | Most cohousing | Any |
| Community Land Trust | Land held in perpetuity, members lease | Dancing Rabbit, Earthaven, Findhorn CBS | Any |
| Internal currency | Complementary currency alongside national | Damanhur (Credito at 1:1 with euro) | 200+ |
| Revenue diversification | Multiple streams (the pattern ALL survivors share) | Every survivor | Any |

## What Living Collective Needs Next (Priority Order)

1. **Define the economic engine** — What does the community produce and sell? Agriculture? Education/retreats? A manufactured product? Remote work collective? Must answer before land.
2. **Write operational governance** — Not the poetic vision (that exists). The bylaws: decision process, quorum, conflict resolution steps 1-2-3-4, financial approval thresholds, membership admission/departure.
3. **Design membership pipeline** — Visit (1-3 weeks) → provisional membership (3-6 months) → full membership vote. Svanholm attributes survival to this.
4. **Establish legal entity** — Community Benefit Society (Findhorn model) or CLT. Enables democratic share offerings for fundraising.
5. **Start producing before land** — What economic activity can begin NOW? Svanholm started farming immediately. Twin Oaks started making hammocks. Build economic capability while the community forms.

## On Sacha Stone / New Earth Specifically

The New Earth Exchange and sovereignty vision resonate deeply with what the Living Collective is building. The VALUES align. The EXECUTION does not. Tennessee has bare land, no plan, NDAs, and $10K-$100K membership fees that buy access not equity. Bali is an event venue not a community. Mexico and Africa are announcements only.

**What to keep**: Zero-point economics philosophy, custodianship principle, sovereignty as foundation, art as architecture, biological ascension as vitality deepening.

**What to learn from**: Don't start with membership fees before a plan. Don't concentrate ownership with a founder. Don't use NDAs. Build before selling. Transparency over mystique.

**The Living Collective can be what New Earth talks about but hasn't built.**
