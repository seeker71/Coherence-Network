# Governance Framework

> No rules, resonance — but resonance needs structure to hold it. This is the operational governance document. Not the poetry (that exists in the concepts). The bylaws.

## Core Principle

Governance follows the organism metaphor: cells sense, the field responds, decisions emerge. But unlike a biological organism, a human community needs explicit agreements to handle the cases where sensing fails, conflict arises, or power concentrates.

**The Damanhur lesson**: 50 years, 600 people, 30 communities — their constitution evolved from 130+ articles down to 15 core principles. Start simple, add when needed, never more than necessary.

## Decision-Making Process

### Level 1: Neighborhood (10-50 people)
- **Method**: Consent-based (sociocratic). Not consensus (everyone agrees) but consent (no one has a principled objection).
- **Frequency**: Weekly circle, 90 minutes max
- **Quorum**: 60% of members present (or proxy submitted)
- **Facilitation**: Rotating facilitator, trained in consent process. New facilitator every month.
- **Documentation**: Decisions recorded in shared log within 24 hours

### Level 2: Village (50-200 people)
- **Method**: Sociocratic circle delegation. Each neighborhood sends 2 delegates.
- **Frequency**: Monthly circle
- **Quorum**: All neighborhoods represented
- **Scope**: Cross-neighborhood decisions (shared infrastructure, budget, new members, external relations)

### Level 3: Network (200+ people / multiple villages)
- **Method**: Federation. Each village sends delegates.
- **Frequency**: Quarterly gathering
- **Scope**: Inter-village resource sharing, land acquisition, network economics

### Fallback When Consent Fails
1. **Extended round**: facilitator calls a second sensing round with 24-hour pause
2. **Small group**: the 2-3 people with strongest objections meet with a mediator
3. **Supermajority**: if small group can't resolve, 75% vote overrides (used sparingly, tracked)
4. **External mediation**: professional mediator brought in. Community pays. Cost shared.

## Conflict Resolution (Steps 1-2-3-4)

| Step | When | Who | Method | Timeline |
|------|------|-----|--------|----------|
| 1. Direct | First sign of tension | The two people | Direct conversation, ideally same day | 1-3 days |
| 2. Mediated | Direct didn't resolve | + 1 trained mediator (internal) | NVC-based mediation session | 1 week |
| 3. Circle | Mediation didn't resolve | Neighborhood circle | Restorative circle, all voices heard | 2 weeks |
| 4. External | Circle didn't resolve | Professional mediator (external) | Formal mediation, binding if agreed | 1 month |

**Hard rule**: No gossip. If you have an issue with someone, you speak to them directly (Step 1) before talking to anyone else. This single rule prevents 80% of community-destroying dynamics.

## Stewardship Roles (Not Leadership)

Stewardship is rotating service, not power. No role held longer than 12 months without re-consent from the circle.

| Role | Scope | Term | Selection |
|------|-------|------|-----------|
| Circle facilitator | Runs weekly/monthly circles | 1 month, rotating | Volunteered, trained |
| Steward of land | Land council, water, trees, wildlife | 6 months | Consent of land circle |
| Steward of hearth | Kitchen, meals, food systems | 6 months | Consent of nourishment circle |
| Steward of shelter | Buildings, maintenance, new construction | 6 months | Consent of shelter circle |
| Steward of treasury | Financial transparency, common fund | 6 months | Consent of full circle |
| Steward of welcoming | Visitor program, membership pipeline | 6 months | Consent of full circle |
| Steward of network | External relations, inter-community | 6 months | Consent of full circle |

## Financial Governance

- **All finances visible**: dashboard showing income, expenses, reserves, accessible to every member
- **Spending thresholds**:
  - Under $100: any steward, no approval needed
  - $100-$1,000: steward + 1 other steward approval
  - $1,000-$10,000: neighborhood circle consent
  - Over $10,000: full village circle consent
- **Annual budget**: proposed by steward of treasury, consented by full circle, reviewed quarterly
- **Emergency fund**: minimum 6 months operating expenses maintained at all times

## Constitutional Principles (15, following Damanhur's evolution)

1. Every member is sovereign. No one compels. Resonance, not obligation.
2. The land is custodied, not owned. It belongs to the field.
3. Decisions emerge through consent, not authority.
4. Conflict is addressed directly, never through gossip.
5. All finances are transparent to all members.
6. No role is held longer than 12 months without re-consent.
7. New members enter through the welcoming pipeline (visit → provisional → full).
8. Departing members leave with dignity and whatever personal property they brought.
9. The community produces something of value beyond its own sustenance.
10. Children are the responsibility of the whole field, not just their parents.
11. Elders are at the center, not the periphery.
12. Beauty is a daily practice, not an occasional luxury.
13. The community budgets at ~150 people. At capacity, it buds a new neighborhood or village.
14. The field connects to the network. No community is an island.
15. This constitution evolves by consent of the full circle. No principle is permanent.

## Training Required Before Community Launch

- [ ] 3+ members trained in NVC (Nonviolent Communication)
- [ ] 2+ members trained in sociocratic facilitation
- [ ] 1+ member trained in restorative justice circles
- [ ] All founding members complete a 2-day governance workshop together
- [ ] Written conflict resolution procedure agreed and signed by all founders
