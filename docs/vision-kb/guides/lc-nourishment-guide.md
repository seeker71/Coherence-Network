# Nourishment -- Practical Guide

> See [concept story](../concepts/lc-nourishment.md) for the living frequency behind this work.

## Feeding 50 People: The Math

50 people need ~100,000 calories/day (~36.5 million/year). A well-designed permaculture system produces 10-15 million calories/acre/year with calorie-dense crops. You need 3-5 acres intensive production + 2-5 acres food forest/orchard/pasture.

### Caloric Backbone

| Crop | Calories/acre/yr | Role | Notes |
|------|-----------------|------|-------|
| Potatoes | 15-18M | Primary starch | 20K-40K lbs/acre possible |
| Sweet potatoes | 12-15M | Starch + vitamin A | Stores 4-6 months |
| Winter squash | 4-6M | Starch + storage | Butternut stores 6+ months |
| Dry beans | 2-4M | Primary protein | 1,500 lbs/acre |
| Corn (dry) | 6-8M | Starch + animal feed | Grind for cornmeal/polenta |
| Jerusalem artichoke | 8-10M | Starch, inulin | Zero maintenance once established |
| Sunflowers | 3-5M | Oil + protein | Press for cooking oil |

### Protein Sources

| Source | Yield | Notes |
|--------|-------|-------|
| Dry beans/lentils (1 acre) | 1,200-1,800 lbs | Protein for 50 people for 3-4 months |
| Eggs (50 laying hens) | 30-40 eggs/day at peak | 15-20 lbs protein/week |
| Goat milk/cheese (10 dairy goats) | 5-8 gal/day | Protein, fat, calcium |
| Nuts (20 mature walnut/chestnut trees) | 1,000-2,000 lbs/yr | 50-100 lbs/tree |
| Fish (quarter-acre pond) | 300-500 lbs/yr | Tilapia or catfish |

### Land Allocation for Year-Round Feeding

| Zone | Acres | Purpose |
|------|-------|---------|
| Intensive annual vegetables | 2 | Potatoes, beans, squash, greens, roots |
| Food forest + orchard | 2 | Nut trees, fruit trees, berries, perennial vegetables |
| Grain or corn | 1 | Bread, polenta, animal feed |
| Pasture/forage | 0.5 | Goats and chickens |
| Ponds | 0.25 | Fish and irrigation |
| **Total** | **~6** | Plus surrounding land for habitat, timber, wildness |

## The Food Forest

### Seven Layers

| Layer | Height | Examples |
|-------|--------|---------|
| Canopy | 30-60 ft | Walnut, chestnut, honey locust, oak |
| Understory | 10-30 ft | Apple, pear, plum, cherry, hazelnut, persimmon |
| Shrub | 3-10 ft | Currant, gooseberry, elderberry, blueberry, aronia |
| Herbaceous | 0-3 ft | Comfrey, sorrel, rhubarb, lovage, artichoke |
| Ground cover | 0-6 in | Strawberry, clover, creeping thyme, nasturtium |
| Root | Below ground | Garlic, horseradish, sunchoke, groundnut |
| Vine | Climbing | Grape, kiwi, hops, passionflower |

### Pioneer Species (Year 1)

- **Black locust**: fixes 75-150 lbs N/acre/yr, grows 6-8 ft/yr, coppices for firewood
- **Autumn olive**: N-fixer, edible berries year 2 (invasive in eastern US -- use alternatives)
- **Comfrey** (Bocking 14): deep taproots mine minerals, cut 4-5x/yr, 100+ tons biomass/acre/yr
- **White clover**: living mulch, N-fixer, bee forage
- **Daikon radish**: breaks compacted subsoil, winter-kills, leaves organic channels

### Timeline: Bare Land to Productive Forest

| Year | What Happens | What You Harvest |
|------|-------------|-----------------|
| 0 | Observe. Soil test. Plan. | Nothing -- learning |
| 1 | Plant pioneers, sheet mulch, dig swales, plant fruit trees | Comfrey biomass, clover for bees |
| 2 | Add berries, herbs, ground covers | Herbs, some berries, pioneer fruit |
| 3 | Understory filling in, canopy 10-15 ft | First real fruit (currants, gooseberries), herbs, greens |
| 4 | Berry bushes full production, fruit trees bearing | 200-500 lbs berries, first apples/pears |
| 5 | Visibly forest-like, shade-tolerant species thriving | 500-1,000 lbs fruit, consistent berries |
| 6 | Minimal maintenance, chop-and-drop mulching | Full berry/fruit production, first walnut/chestnut |
| 7+ | Mature. 2-4 hrs/week/acre maintenance | Thousands of pounds diverse food annually |

### Species by Climate Zone

**Temperate (zones 5-7)**: Canopy: black walnut, heartnut, chestnut, honey locust. Understory: apple (Liberty, Enterprise, Freedom), pear, tart cherry, plum, persimmon, pawpaw. Shrub: currant, gooseberry, elderberry, aronia, hazelnut, serviceberry.

**Subtropical (zones 8-10)**: Canopy: pecan, macadamia, moringa, carob. Understory: fig, pomegranate, citrus, loquat, avocado, mulberry, olive. Shrub: blueberry, Barbados cherry, guava, feijoa.

**Arid/Mediterranean**: Canopy: pistachio, almond, carob, mesquite. Understory: fig, pomegranate, olive, jujube, prickly pear. Shrub: rosemary, lavender, goji berry, saltbush.

**Cold continental (zones 3-4)**: Canopy: Manchurian walnut, Siberian pine nut, birch. Understory: Honeycrisp apple, sour cherry, plum, nanking cherry. Shrub: honeyberry/haskap, highbush cranberry, sea buckthorn, saskatoon.

## Kitchen and Preservation

### Community Kitchen Layout (500-800 sqft for 50 people)

- **Wood-fired clay oven** (36-42 in chamber): bakes 15-20 loaves, roasts, slow cooks overnight
- **Rocket mass stove**: 6-burner equivalent, primary daily cooking
- **Prep island**: 4x8 ft minimum, 6 people chopping simultaneously
- **Two deep sinks** + handwash
- **Walk-in pantry**: 80-120 sqft for bulk dry storage
- **Cool room**: 50-55F for cheese aging, fermentation, vegetable storage
- **Long table**: visible from cooking area

Proportions: 5-gal pot of soup feeds 40 with bread. 10 lbs beans (soaked overnight) feeds 50. 20 lbs potatoes roasted in clay oven = a meal.

### Fermentation

| Product | Ingredients | Time | Yield | Shelf Life |
|---------|------------|------|-------|------------|
| Sauerkraut | Cabbage + 2% salt | 2-6 wk | 50 lbs cabbage = 40 lbs kraut | 1 yr+ cool |
| Kimchi | Napa cabbage, radish, ginger, chili | 1-4 wk | 50 lbs = ~45 lbs | 6-12 mo refrigerated |
| Miso | Soybeans + koji + salt | 6-18 mo | 10 lbs beans = ~20 lbs | Years |
| Sourdough starter | Flour + water | 7-10 days | Feeds itself indefinitely | Immortal |
| Kombucha | Sweet tea + SCOBY | 7-14 days | 5-gal batches | Continuous |
| Vinegar | Wine/cider/fruit scraps | 2-6 mo | 1 gal wine = ~1 gal vinegar | Indefinite |
| Yogurt/kefir | Milk + culture | 12-24 hr | 1:1 | 2-3 weeks |
| Lacto hot sauce | Peppers + salt + garlic | 1-4 wk | 10 lbs peppers = ~10 pints | 1 yr+ |

**Annual targets for 50 people**: 200-300 lbs sauerkraut, 100-150 lbs kimchi, 50-100 lbs miso. Two afternoons of communal chopping.

### Drying

Solar dehydrator from scrap wood + glass: under $50.

- Tomatoes: 20 lbs fresh = ~2 lbs dried
- Herbs: hang in bundles, one season = one year's supply
- Apples: slice thin, dry until leathery (200-500 lbs per mature tree)
- Hot peppers: string as ristras
- Beans: dry on vine, thresh, store with bay leaves (2-5 yr shelf life)

| Tomato sauce/salsa | 100-150 qt | Water bath |
| Fruit preserves | 50-100 pt | Water bath |

### Root Cellar

Dig into north-facing hillside. 35-40F, 85-95% humidity. 100-150 sqft for 50 people. Cost: $500-2,000.

| Crop | Storage Life |
|------|-------------|
| Potatoes | 4-6 months |
| Carrots, beets, turnips (damp sand) | 4-5 months |
| Winter squash (50-55F preferred) | 3-6 months |
| Cabbage (wrap in newspaper) | 3-4 months |
| Apples (away from potatoes) | 2-4 months |
| Onions, garlic (mesh bags, airflow) | 6-9 months |

## Water for Growing

| Use | Volume |
|-----|--------|
| Annual vegetable garden | 1 in/wk = ~27,000 gal/acre/season |
| 50 chickens | 10-15 gal/day |
| 10 goats | 15-20 gal/day |
| **Total food-production water (50 people)** | **500K-800K gal/growing season** |

### Rainwater from Roofs (30 in rain/yr)

- 2,000 sqft barn roof: 36,000 gal/yr
- 1,500 sqft community building: 27,000 gal/yr
- Four 800 sqft residences: 57,600 gal/yr
- **Total from roofs**: ~120,000 gal/yr

Storage: IBC totes ($50-100 used), ferrocement cisterns ($0.50-1.00/gal capacity), poly tanks.

### Swales

Level ditches on contour. 100-ft swale, 2 ft deep holds ~1,500 gal after 1 in rain. Trees on swale berms grow 2-3x faster. Build with A-frame level ($0), 18-24 in deep, 36-48 in wide.

### Ponds

Quarter-acre pond (~100x100 ft, 6-8 ft deep) = 500,000 gal. Serves as irrigation reserve, fish production (300-500 lbs/yr), duck habitat, fire suppression, microclimate moderator, wildlife habitat. Cost: $3K-8K excavation.

### Drip Irrigation

30-50% less water than overhead. 1-acre system: $500-1,500. Connect to cisterns via gravity or small solar pump.

## First Year Food Plan (Temperate Zones 5-7)

### Before Day One

- Soil test: $15-30 via county extension
- Bulk food buy: 500 lbs beans, 300 lbs rice, 200 lbs flour, 50 lbs salt, 20 gal oil, 50 lbs oats, spices. Cost: $800-1,200
- Seed order: $200-400 from regional companies (Fedco, Johnny's, Territorial, Baker Creek, Southern Exposure)
- Tools: 6 digging forks, 4 shovels, 3 wheelbarrows, 2 broadforks, hoes, rakes, scythe, seed trays, potting mix. $1,000-2,000

### Month-by-Month

| Month | Key Activities | Harvest |
|-------|---------------|---------|
| Jan-Feb | Seed starting indoors (onions, leeks, celery, peppers, eggplant 8-10 wk before last frost). Order bare-root fruit trees. Build chicken coop. Compost kitchen scraps | None |
| Mar | Start tomatoes indoors. Start brassicas. Direct sow peas, spinach, lettuce, radishes. Plant garlic, potatoes | None |
| Apr | Transplant brassicas. Sow beets, carrots, turnips, chard. Plant fruit trees/berries. Sheet mulch food forest area. Set up beehives | Radishes, first greens |
| May | Transplant tomatoes, peppers, squash. Sow beans, corn, sunflowers, basil. Mulch everything 4-6 in. Install drip irrigation. Plant comfrey, rhubarb, asparagus | Spring peas, lettuce, radishes |
| Jun | Succession sow lettuce, beans every 2-3 wk. First strawberries. First goat kids/milk | Salads, peas, herbs, green onions |
| Jul-Aug | Harvest daily. Begin preservation (can, ferment, dry). Second planting fall crops. Harvest potatoes | Full summer abundance |
| Sep-Oct | Harvest winter squash, sweet potatoes, dry beans. Major fermentation (200+ lbs kraut, 100+ lbs kimchi). Canning marathon. Plant garlic. Cover crop bare beds. Stock root cellar | Pantry visibly full |
| Nov-Dec | Eat from stores. Cold frames extend greens. Repair tools. Seed saving. Plan year two | Stored food + root cellar |

### Feeding Progression

| Timeframe | Primary Source |
|-----------|---------------|
| Month 1-2 | Bulk purchased staples + first greens |
| Month 3-4 | Spring greens, peas, radishes + purchased |
| Month 5-6 | Full summer garden + eggs + purchased grains/oils |
| Month 7-9 | Garden abundance + preservation |
| Month 10-12 | Stored food + root cellar + ferments + purchased |
| Year 2 | Garden: 40-60% of calories |
| Year 3 | 50-70% from land. Goat milk, cheese, significant honey |
| Year 5 | Food forest in full production. 70-85% self-provisioning |
| Year 7+ | Mature system. 80-90% from the land |
