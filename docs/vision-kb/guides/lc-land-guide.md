# Land Embedding -- Practical Guide

> See [concept story](../concepts/lc-land.md) for the living frequency behind this work.

![A small group of people walking across an open meadow toward a forested hillside, examining the land together](visuals:photorealistic group of five diverse people walking across a rolling meadow in early morning, examining the landscape, one kneeling to touch the soil, another looking at a creek in the middle distance, forested hills behind them, wildflowers in the grass, a sense of possibility and careful attention, golden hour light, mountains on the horizon)

## How to Start

### Forming the Core Group

Three to seven people who share resonance deeply enough for a decades-long commitment. Meet in person. Cook together. Camp on raw land in bad weather. The people who stay through a rainy weekend with no electricity are your core.

### Scouting Checklist

Visit at least five properties. Visit in every season. Walk the boundaries. Bring someone who knows water, soil, and building. Walk in silence.

### Non-Negotiables

| Factor | What to Look For | Test/Cost |
|--------|-----------------|-----------|
| **Water** | Year-round spring or creek; well 5+ GPM; rain catchment potential | Water quality test: $50-150 |
| **Soil** | Dark topsoil >12 in = good history; clay at 6 in = building material; sand = heavy composting needed | County extension: $15-30/sample (pH, organic matter, nutrients, contamination) |
| **Aspect/slope** | South-facing = 40% more solar energy (N hemisphere); 2-8% slope ideal; 15%+ needs terracing | Solar Pathfinder tool: ~$250 |
| **Road access** | Year-round vehicle access; maintained county road preferred | Private road: $20K-80K/mi to build, $2K-5K/yr maintenance |
| **Existing infrastructure** | Habitable structure, well, septic, electrical hookup each saves $15K-50K | Old barns = immediate tool/material shelter |
| **Zoning** | Rural agricultural (A-1, AG) most permissive; some counties have no zoning | Talk to county planning office + neighbors |
| **History** | Industrial ag = pesticide residue; old orchard = graftable rootstock; mining = heavy metals | Title search + neighbor interviews |

### How Much Land

| Community Size | Minimum | Comfortable | Notes |
|---------------|---------|-------------|-------|
| Per person | 0.5 acre | 1 acre | Permaculture-intensity, not conventional ag |
| 50 people | 25 acres | 50 acres | Includes food, forest, commons, wildlife |
| 100 people | 50 acres | 100 acres | Add more if livestock beyond chickens/goats |

## Custodianship Models

| Model | How It Works | Protection Level | Setup Cost |
|-------|-------------|-----------------|------------|
| **Community Land Trust (CLT)** | Nonprofit holds land in perpetuity; 99-year ground leases; 1/3 residents, 1/3 community, 1/3 public board | Strongest -- land permanently off market | $5K-15K legal |
| **Housing Cooperative** | Members jointly own entity; one member one vote; dissolution protections recommended | Moderate -- supermajority could theoretically sell | Lower than CLT |
| **Conservation Easement** | Restricts development permanently; reduces value 30-60% (lower taxes, tax deduction) | Permanent -- survives all future sales | Varies by land trust |
| **Sovereign Land** | Indigenous/religious/recognized community status outside standard property system | Deepest | Requires specific status |

**Practical layering**: CLT holds land + individual 99-year ground leases + conservation easement on wildlands + cooperative manages shared infrastructure. Attorneys specializing in community land can assemble in 3-6 months. 300+ CLTs operate in the US. Start: [Grounded Solutions CLT Startup Hub](https://groundedsolutions.org/resources/start-upclthub/).

## Climate-Specific Guidance

### Temperate (Europe, NE USA, NZ, Pacific Northwest)

| Aspect | Details |
|--------|---------|
| **Growing season** | 150-200 days; four distinct seasons |
| **Food forest** | Canopy: chestnut, walnut, hazelnut, oak. Understory: apple, pear, plum, cherry. Shrub: currant, gooseberry, elderberry. Ground: comfrey, rhubarb, strawberry, clover. 3-5 yr to establish, 15 yr to maturity |
| **Building** | Cob: $50-150/sqft owner-built. Straw bale: R-30+ insulation. Timber frame + earthen infill. Living roofs |
| **Water** | 30-50 in/yr rainfall. Swales on contour. 2,000 sqft roof captures 37,000 gal/yr at 30 in rain. Sepp Holzer retention ponds |
| **Energy** | Solar good summer, drops 60-80% winter. Rocket mass heater burns 1/5 the wood. Plan 3-5 cords/household/winter |
| **Season extension** | 30x96 ft hoop house: $3K-8K, feeds 10-15 people greens through winter |

### Tropical (Costa Rica, Bali, Thailand, Colombia, Hawaii)

| Aspect | Details |
|--------|---------|
| **Rhythm** | No winter; wet/dry seasons; year-round growth |
| **Food forest** | Productive in 18 months. Canopy: breadfruit, jackfruit, coconut, mango, avocado. Understory: banana, papaya, cacao, coffee, citrus. Shrub: cassava, taro, moringa. 1 acre feeds 8-12 people (Ernst Gotsch syntropic model) |
| **Building** | Bamboo: $30-80/sqft SE Asia, $60-150/sqft C America. Borax-treated = 20-yr durability. Elevated 1-3 ft. Deep overhangs 4-6 ft. Cross-ventilation replaces AC |
| **Water** | 60-200 in/yr rainfall. Manage excess. Ferrocement cistern 5,000 gal: $500-2,000 |
| **Energy** | Solar abundant. Micro-hydro: 10 ft head + 10 GPM = ~500W continuous. Biogas from organic waste. No heating needed |
| **Considerations** | Insect management (screens, neem, citronella). Fast decomposition. Complex land tenure (ejido, customary) -- hire local attorney |

### Arid (SW USA, Spain, Australia, North Africa, Middle East)

| Aspect | Details |
|--------|---------|
| **Rhythm** | Water scarcity + extreme temp swings (40F daily range). Brief intense rains |
| **Food** | Brad Lancaster: 11 in/yr rainfall, 100K+ gal/yr from earthworks. Sunken waffle gardens (Zuni). Anchor trees: date, pomegranate, fig, olive, jujube, mesquite, prickly pear, moringa |
| **Building** | Earthships: rammed-earth tire walls, 65-72F year-round, zero energy. Rammed earth/adobe. $75-200/sqft owner-built |
| **Water** | Every surface = catchment. Greywater recycling essential. 50 people = ~2,500 gal/day greywater. Composting toilets save 25-30% water. 10,000 sqft catchment + 12 in rain = 75,000 gal/yr |
| **Energy** | SW USA: 6+ peak sun hours. 50 people at 5 kWh/person/day = 50 kW array (~120 panels, 2,000 sqft). Earth-tube cooling. Passive solar |

### Mediterranean (Portugal, Greece, S France, California, Chile)

| Aspect | Details |
|--------|---------|
| **Rhythm** | Wet mild winters, hot dry summers. Double growing season (spring+autumn) |
| **Food** | Ancient trinity: olive, grape, fig + almond, pomegranate, citrus, carob. Drought-adapted understory: rosemary, lavender, thyme, oregano, sage. Drip-irrigated summer garden |
| **Building** | Thick-walled rammed earth/stone/cob/adobe. White-washed exterior. Courtyard design. Deep overhangs. 3,000 years of passive design precedent |
| **Water** | 15-30 in/yr, almost all in winter. Every drop captured. Tamera model: water retention landscapes restored year-round springs in 15 years. 50 people need 20K-30K gal/mo stored for summer |
| **Fire** | 30-100 ft defensible space. Fire-resistant materials (earth, stone, metal roofs). Irrigated gardens as green belts. Goat browsing keeps understory low |

## First Year Timeline

**Months 1-3: Mapping and Testing**
- Walk entire boundary, GPS it
- Soil test: 3-5 samples, $15-30 each (pH, organic matter, nutrients, heavy metals)
- Water mapping: locate every spring, seep, seasonal creek; measure flow; test quality
- Sun mapping: photograph at solar noon monthly (or Solar Pathfinder, ~$250)
- Wind mapping: note prevailing directions by season
- Vegetation survey: identify every tree species
- Meet all neighbors (bring food, listen)
- County: planning office, health dept, fire district (get everything in writing)

**Months 4-6: Shelter and Water**
- Reliable clean water supply: well pump, spring capture, or rainwater. 2,500 gal tank: $800-1,500
- First composting toilet (twin-vault): $200-500 materials. [Humanure Handbook](https://humanurehandbook.com/)
- Three-bin compost system from pallets
- First cover crop on bare soil: crimson clover, winter rye, daikon radish
- Begin tree propagation nursery

**Months 7-9: First Earthworks and Plantings**
- Swales on contour (crew of 5 = 100 ft/day hand-dug)
- First 50-100 trees: nitrogen-fixers (black locust, alder, autumn olive) + pioneers. 6 in mulch ring
- First annual garden: 500-1,000 sqft
- Chickens (20 hens) if ready

**Months 10-12: Community Infrastructure**
- Common gathering space (covered outdoor area, fire pit)
- Weekly rhythm: shared meals, land walks, planning circles
- First community agreements (revisit every season)
- Document everything: monthly photos from same points, rain/temp/harvest logs
- Plan year two

## Technology and Infrastructure

### Energy Sizing for 50 People

| Source | Capacity | Cost | Output |
|--------|----------|------|--------|
| **Solar** | 50 kW (120-150 panels, ~2,500 sqft) | $50K-100K | 200-300 kWh/day most climates |
| **Battery (LFP)** | 750 kWh (3-day autonomy) | $150K-250K | Full autonomy |
| **Battery (starter)** | 250 kWh (1-day + generator backup) | $50K-80K | Essential loads |
| **Micro-hydro** | 1 kW continuous (20 ft head, 20 GPM) | $5K-15K | 24 kWh/day, 24/7 |
| **Wind** | 10 kW turbine | $30K-60K installed | 10K-15K kWh/yr in good site |
| **Biogas** | IBC tote digester | $2K-8K | 3-5 m3 methane/day = 4-6 hrs cooking |

Community living = 3-8 kWh/person/day (vs 30 kWh American average).

### Water Systems

| System | Capacity/Sizing | Cost |
|--------|----------------|------|
| Rainwater (10K sqft roof, 30 in rain) | 180,000 gal/yr | Ferrocement, poly tanks, or lined ponds |
| Greywater wetland (50 people) | 500-1,000 sqft wetland | $3K-8K |
| Composting toilets (community scale) | 2-4 vault units for 50 people | $500-2K/unit |
| Spring (5 GPM) | 7,200 gal/day | Spring box + annual testing |

### Communications

- Starlink/fixed wireless: $100-150/mo for reliable rural internet
- Community mesh network distributes to all structures
- Record everything: rainfall, harvests, energy, soil tests, species. Open-source it

### Funding Models

- **Community shares**: $10K-50K per founding member; 20 members x $25K = $500K for land + initial infra
- **Sweat equity**: Labor at $15-25/hr credited toward share; 1,000 hrs = $15K-25K share
- **Grants**: USDA Rural Development, state ag grants, NRCS EQIP cost-sharing, conservation easement tax deductions (30-60% of land cost)
- **Social enterprise** (year 2+): Workshops ($20K-80K/yr), visitor stays ($30K-100K/yr), farm surplus, remote work, value-added products
- **Crowdfunding/community bonds**: $500-5K loans from supporters, 5-10 yr terms

**What does NOT work**: Single wealthy benefactor (power imbalance), large commercial loans (debt drives extraction), expecting year-one profitability, skipping legal structure.

## Legal Pathways by Country

| Country | Structure | Legal Cost | Key Resource |
|---------|-----------|------------|-------------|
| **United States** | 501(c)(3) CLT + ground leases | $5K-15K | Grounded Solutions, Sustainable Economies Law Center |
| **Portugal** | Cooperativa or associacao; "Quintas" ag zoning | Varies | Tamera model; interior Alentejo $1K-3K/acre |
| **Costa Rica** | S.A. or S.R.L.; fee simple ownership (except Maritime Zone) | Varies | Strong environmental regs support sustainable use |
| **UK/Scotland** | CLT (originated here); Scotland Land Reform Act gives community right-to-buy | Varies | CLT Network UK; Scottish Gov funding |
| **New Zealand** | Incorporated society, charitable trust, or LP | Varies | Overseas Investment Act for large non-resident purchases |
| **SE Asia** | Long-term leases (30-yr renewable); Thai-majority companies; Hak Pakai (Indonesia) | Varies | Always engage local attorney |

### Zoning Strategies

- **Agricultural (A-1, AG)**: Multiple dwellings as farm worker housing
- **Planned Unit Development (PUD)**: Flexible site design, cluster buildings, preserve open space
- **Conservation subdivision**: Higher density on small portion, permanently conserve the rest
- **Incremental development**: Start with what's permitted by right; build trust; apply for permanent later
- **Rural exemptions**: Ag buildings, small structures, buildings without plumbing often exempt

## Exchange and Sovereignty

### What You Produce Locally (Year 3+)

- 70-90% of food
- 100% of water
- 80-100% of energy
- 50-80% of building materials
- Medicinal herbs, basic healthcare, education, fermented/preserved foods, seed, craft goods, culture

### What You Trade For

Metal tools, hardware, glass, plumbing, electrical, vehicles/fuel, medical supplies, communication tech, specialty foods (salt, coffee, spices, oil), legal/accounting, insurance

### Exchange Value Without Extraction

| Stream | Typical Income |

| Workshops (natural building, permaculture) | $20K-80K/yr |

| Farm surplus and value-added products | Variable |
