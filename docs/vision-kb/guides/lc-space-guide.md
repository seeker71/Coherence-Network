# Space -- Practical Guide

> See [concept story](../concepts/lc-space.md) for the living frequency behind this work.

## What to Build First

The sequence follows the body's priorities: shelter, fire and food, ability to gather, then permanent homes.

| Phase | Timeline | What | Cost |
|-------|----------|------|------|
| **The Ground** | Week 1-2 | Temporary shelters (platform tents, yurts) + composting toilets + hand-wash station | $15K-40K |
| **The Hearth** | Month 1-3 | Community kitchen (first permanent structure) + tool storage + outdoor shower + first aid | $8K-25K |
| **The Clearing** | Month 2-4 | Circle of benches around fire pit. No roof, no walls, no permit. Highest-return build | $500-3K |
| **The Sanctuary** | Month 3-6 | First small permanent structure. Thick walls, silence, beauty. Community training project | $3K-8K |
| **The Den** | Month 4-12 | Workshop: timber frame, open south wall, tool storage, natural light. 60-80 sqm | $5K-15K |
| **The Nests** | Month 6-24 | Private dwellings. Only after community has cooked, gathered, sat in silence, and built together | $2K-8K each |
| **Year 2+** | Ongoing | Bathhouse, expanded Den, second Hearth -- emerges as community knows its patterns | Variable |

## Building Methods by Climate

| Method | $/sqft | Best Climate | DIY-ability | Thermal Notes |
|--------|--------|-------------|-------------|--------------|
| **Cob** (clay+sand+straw) | $15-50 | Temperate, Mediterranean | Excellent (bare hands) | R-1-2/ft, high thermal mass. Walls dry 3-6 mo |
| **Straw bale** (+plaster) | $10-30 | Cold/temperate, dry | Good (moderate skill) | R-30 to R-40 for 18" walls. Must stay dry |
| **Rammed earth** | $30-75 | Arid, Mediterranean, temperate | Moderate (needs formwork) | R-0.5/in, exceptional thermal mass |
| **Earthbag/SuperAdobe** | $5-20 | Any, especially arid/seismic | Good (repetitive) | Earthquake/hurricane resistant. Team of 8 builds dome in 2 wk |
| **Bamboo** (treated) | $8-25 | Tropical, subtropical | Good with training | Low mass, excellent ventilation. Borax-treated: 25-50 yr |
| **Timber frame** (+infill) | $25-60 | Temperate, cold, forested | Moderate (some skill) | Depends on infill material |
| **CEB** (compressed earth block) | $8-15 | Arid, Mediterranean, temperate | Good with press | OSE Liberator press (~$4K) = 500 blocks/day |
| **Earthship** (rammed tires+solar) | $20-40 | Any, designed for extremes | Moderate | Near-zero energy heating/cooling |

### Climate-Specific Guidance

**Cold continental**: Straw bale walls (R-40+), timber frame structure, rammed earth interior for mass. South-facing windows. Thick living roofs (8-12 in substrate). $40-75/sqft.

**Temperate maritime**: Cob (born in Devon/Cornwall). Deep overhangs (3-4 ft) for rain. Lime plaster (breathes). Living roofs. Raised foundations. $25-50/sqft. Cob free if clay on site.

**Arid**: Rammed earth/adobe native. 18-24 in walls shift temp peak 10-12 hr. Small east/west windows, large south. Earthbag domes handle 40+ degree daily swings. Courtyards with water = 10-15F cooler. $15-35/sqft.

**Tropical**: Bamboo. Raised floors, open/woven walls for cross-ventilation. Deep thatch/palm roofs. No thermal mass. IBUKU Bali: $15-30/sqft. Treat all bamboo with borax.

**Mixed/transitional**: Hybrid methods. Watch how traditional builders responded before industrial materials.

## The Hearth (Community Kitchen)

### Design for 50 People (80-120 sqm)

| Zone | Size | Contents |
|------|------|----------|
| **Cooking core** | 25-30 sqm | Wood-fired clay oven (bakes 20 loaves). 2x rocket stoves ($200-500 each, 60-80% less fuel than open fire). Clay oven: $500-2K, 2-3 wk build+cure. Propane backup |
| **Prep space** | 15-20 sqm | Stone/hardwood counters at standing height. Deep double sinks, gravity-fed. Open shelving. Fermentation corner (north wall). Herb/garlic hooks from beams |
| **Long table** | 20-30 sqm | Single table seating 40-50. Bench seating both sides. Cook line visible from every seat |
| **Open wall** | South/west | Half-open to garden. Timber counter at threshold. Canvas/wooden shutters for storms |
| **Storage/pantry** | 10-15 sqm | Cool north-facing room. Thick cob/rammed earth walls. Ventilated drying space above. Gravity grain bins |

**Total materials: $8K-25K.** Sweat equity from 20 people over 2-3 months with natural building workshop leader guiding first 2 weeks. Oven and rocket stoves operational within month 1.

## The Clearing (Gathering Space)

### Design for 50 People

- **Footprint**: 12-15 m diameter circle (~150-180 sqm)
- **Fire pit**: Stone-lined, 1m across, 40cm deep, drain at bottom. Iron grate for cooking. $200-500 (free with fieldstone)
- **Bench ring**: 30-40 linear ft. Split-log on stone footings ($100-300, 1 day) to carved stone seats ($2K-5K)
- **Ground**: Packed earth or fine gravel. Slight outward slope for drainage
- **No roof** (essential). Optional canvas shade for summer
- **Acoustics**: Slight bowl shape focuses sound. Low earth berm behind outer ring reflects sound. No amplification needed

**Total: $500-3,000. Build time: 2-5 days with 10 people.** Highest-return build in the entire community.

## The Nest (Private Dwellings)

### Minimum Viable Private Space: 15-25 sqm per person/couple

**Cluster design**: Groups of 4-6 around shared courtyard/garden. Each cluster shares bathhouse, outdoor tea station, covered porch. Dunbar's nested circles: Nest-cluster-community = family-neighborhood-village.

**Privacy gradients**: Wide common path -> cluster courtyard -> narrow personal entry. Sound isolation: 18 in cob or straw bale between sleeping spaces.

**Shared walls**: Two Nests sharing a wall = 30-40% less material than freestanding.

**Each Nest needs**: Built-in sleeping platform (storage beneath). Round window facing chosen view. Small wood stove or rocket mass heater ($200-400 to build, heats 8-12 hr). One outlet, one light. Deep wall niche. A door that closes.

**Cost per Nest: $2K-8K.** 15-20 Nests for 50 people: $30K-120K over months 6-24.

## The Sanctuary (Stillness Space)

- **Footprint**: 15-25 sqm (holds 12-15 sitting)
- **Walls**: 45-60 cm thick rammed earth, cob, or earthbag (swallows sound)
- **Shape**: Circular/oval. Domed/vaulted ceiling. Single round skylight at apex. Narrow curving entrance
- **Floor**: Packed earth or stone, heated from below (rocket mass flue in serpentine channels)
- **Materials cost: $3K-8K.** Build time: 4-8 weeks with crew of 6-10
- **Training project**: Small enough to complete in 1-2 months. Teaches every skill for later builds

## Infrastructure Layout (50 People on 15-20 Acres)

Concentric rings radiating from the Hearth, privacy increasing outward.

| Ring | Size | What | Spacing |
|------|------|------|---------|
| **The Core** | 0.5-1 acre | Hearth (center), Clearing (30-50m away), Sanctuary (within 100m), Den (earshot of Hearth) | All within 2-min walk |
| **Nesting Ring** | 2-4 acres | 3-4 clusters of 4-6 Nests on curving path | 100-200m from Hearth, clusters 50-100m apart |
| **Growing Ring** | 3-6 acres | Food forest, annual gardens, greenhouse, chicken coop, goat paddock, compost | Best soil, best sun |
| **Wild Edge** | 5-15 acres | Managed forest, wildlife corridors, walking trails, creek, riparian buffer | Tended but undeveloped |

**Paths**: No straight lines. Follow contour. Main: 1.5m wide, gravel/packed earth. Secondary: 0.5-1m, mown grass. Surface changes signal public-to-private transition.

**Vehicles**: Shared parking at settlement edge, screened by trees. No vehicles in core or nesting rings. Gravel service road loops perimeter.

**Water infrastructure**: Rainwater from every roof into shared cisterns (5K-20K gal). Gravity-fed distribution. Greywater through constructed wetlands to irrigation. Composting toilets throughout. $10K-30K.

**Power**: Community microgrid. 15-30 kW solar, battery for 3-day autonomy. $30K-80K off-grid, less grid-tied.

### Site Reading (Before Placing Any Structure)

- **Water flow**: Where does rain collect? Springs? Flood lines? Build above. Kitchen garden near best water. Composting toilets 50m+ from and downhill of water
- **Sun path**: South-facing slopes get 20-40% more energy. Orient Hearth and Nests south
- **Wind**: Winter prevailing wind -> place buildings on sheltered side
- **Soil**: Test for building suitability (15-30% clay, 70-85% sand/aggregate). Foundation soil becomes walls

## Costs and Timeline

### Phase Breakdown

| Phase | Timeline | Cost | Items |
|-------|----------|------|-------|
| **Ground** | Month 1-3 | $15K-40K | 15-20 yurts/platform tents, 4-6 composting toilets, hand-wash/shower, tool shed, site prep |
| **Heart** | Month 2-6 | $12K-35K | Hearth ($8K-25K), Clearing ($500-3K), Sanctuary ($3K-8K) |
| **Settling** | Month 6-18 | $40K-120K | Den ($5K-15K), 15-20 Nests ($30K-100K), 3-4 cluster bathhouses ($5K-15K) |
| **Roots** | Month 12-24 | $15K-60K | Water infra ($10K-30K), solar microgrid ($30K-80K), bathhouse ($5K-15K), food forest ($3K-10K) |

### Budget Levels

| Level | Total | What You Get |
|-------|-------|-------------|
| **Lean ($50K)** | 18-24 mo | Temporary shelters, outdoor kitchen, clearing, sanctuary, 8-10 cob nests, composting toilets, basic solar |
| **Moderate ($100K)** | 18-24 mo | Above + proper Hearth, workshop, 15 nests, cluster bathhouses, 10 kW solar, cisterns |
| **Comfortable ($200K)** | 24 mo | Full build: all structures, 20 nests, 4 bathhouses, 20 kW solar+battery, complete water, food forest |

50 people contributing 20 hrs/wk for 1 year = 26,000 person-hours. At 10 sqft/person-hour = 260,000 sqft of wall, floor, roof.
