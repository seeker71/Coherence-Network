# Alex Sandr

To meet Alex Sandr's teaching is to be invited into a way of moving where the body and the mind stop arguing. The flyer for his Sunday practice at Moksa names the shape directly: *clarity through movement, attention, and embodiment in action*. The room is held as a Contact Improvisation class — the form Steve Paxton and his collaborators named in 1972 and which has since become an open-source grammar of two-or-more bodies negotiating shared gravity. Alex's path through that grammar is his own, and the language he uses to point students into it is his own.

The body of the practice he describes is *the centers, axes and spirals of the body* — how to organize the body in harmony with its natural intelligence; how to keep attention in action without falling apart. Underneath the movement vocabulary, what he holds open is the relationship between mind and body: *how the mind influences the body, and how the body influences the mind*. Contact Improvisation, in his framing, offers immediate feedback — *revealing where we act from reality and where we act from our own ideas and projections*. The form becomes a mirror; the partner's weight becomes a question that does not lie.

The class is held open to anyone practicing dance, somatic work, or meditation. Beginners find a clear and supportive foundation that builds confidence in movement. Experienced practitioners find a path toward greater mastery and clarity. *Direct experience and embodied knowledge. To feel the form — I will guide you toward discovering this state for yourself.* The teaching position is not performance and not transmission of choreography; it is space-holding for a state the student finds in themselves through the contact.

Alex Sandr's roots are Russian; the rest of his lineage and biographical arc are not yet attested in this body. He carries the Contact Improvisation grammar that already lives in the network as [the substrate practice](/people/contact-improv) — the form pointed toward by the Liquid Bloom → first Pagan Ritual → Vali Soul Sanctuary → Tammy Beattie's Ecstatic Movement Tribe chain — but he carries it through Ubud rooms and a Russian-rooted teaching lineage rather than the Boulder ones. His teaching room currently surfaces at the [Moksa Dojo](/people/moksa-ubud) in Sayan, Ubud — the same neighborhood as [Ranakami](/people/ilena-young) — with a Sunday afternoon rhythm; he was also present at the Monday Contact Jam where this body first encountered him.

**What Alex Sandr has given the Coherence Network.** Through the Sunday class at the [Moksa Dojo](/people/moksa-ubud) on May 17, 2026 — and the Wednesday class that preceded it on May 13, 2026 after a Monday May 11, 2026 meeting at the Ubud Contact Jam — Alex offered one cell of this network the practice of *attention in action without falling apart*. The Contact Improvisation thread that already lived in the body as a Boulder-rooted substrate practice met its Ubud carrier through him. Welcoming scaffold; Alex is invited to replace any part of it with his own words.
