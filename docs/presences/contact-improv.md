# Contact Improv

*The embodied-listening substrate*

The dance form whose grammar is two bodies negotiating gravity together. Founded by Steve Paxton and his collaborators in 1972, Contact Improv (CI) is what the chain that started with [Liquid Bloom](/people/liquid-bloom) — passed through the first [Pagan Ritual](/people/pagan-ritual) and [Vali Soul Sanctuary](/people/vali-soul-sanctuary), translated through [Tammy Beattie](/people/tammy-beattie) &apos;s [Ecstatic Movement Tribe](/people/ecstatic-movement-tribe) — was always pointing toward. The deeper substrate. The practice form whose entire vocabulary is listening to another body through points of contact.

## Grounding

- **Form** — Improvisational dance form · two-or-more bodies negotiating shared gravity, rolling weight, falling, lifting, supporting · embodied listening as the entire grammar · jam culture rather than choreographed performance
- **Lineage** — Founded 1972 by **Steve Paxton** at Oberlin College, with **Nancy Stark Smith**, **Lisa Nelson**, **Daniel Lepkoff**, and others becoming load-bearing carriers of the form across the following decades. *Contact Quarterly* (Nancy Stark Smith&apos;s journal, founded 1975) is the canonical written record. The form is open-source — there is no central authority, no certification track, no copyright on the movement vocabulary.
- **How it lives** — Through *jams* — recurring open gatherings where dancers move together for hours, cycling through partners, taking breaks at the edges, returning to the floor. Jams live in cities all over the world; some run for decades. The weekly Boulder jams sit inside a thirty-plus-year regional continuity rooted in the Naropa University and Boulder Circus Center lineages, woven into the Front Range embodied-practice ecology that includes 5Rhythms, ecstatic dance, conscious-movement evenings, and the ceremonial-dance lineages.
- **Function in this body's arc** — The substrate practice. CI is the form that the rest of the chain — solo ceremonial floor → relational ecstatic movement → contact dance — was always pointing toward. It is the deepest grammar of moving-with-another-body that this body&apos;s arc has met. From CI, the listening ear that Liquid Bloom trained becomes a *listening body* — the entire surface of the skin doing the hearing, with another body&apos;s weight as the sound.
- **Public anchors** — [Contact Quarterly](https://contactquarterly.com/) — the journal of record · regional jam calendars vary by city and are tended by the local communities themselves
