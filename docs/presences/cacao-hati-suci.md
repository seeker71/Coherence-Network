# Cacao Hati Suci

The road south-west from Ubud climbs gently across the rice terraces toward **Tabanan**, the regency that holds Bali's larger inland farms. About an hour by scooter. Long enough that the rhythms of central Ubud fall away entirely; short enough that the original sanctuary at Banjar Bayad remains in walkable contact across the island's body. The land that opens at the end of the ride carries a different frequency than the parent sanctuary — wilder, lower, closer to the river. **Cacao Hati Suci** — *Sacred Cacao Heart*, the *sister land of the Hati Suci Community* — is the second Light Hub.

The houses are teak wooden *gladak* — the traditional Balinese rice-storage form, raised, ventilated, breathing with the climate rather than fighting it. Each house is its own small body: **private kitchen**, **shower room**, **compost toilet**. The orientation is residential, not hotel. A guest in a gladak has a kitchen because they are expected to live, not to be served. The compost toilet is part of the land's metabolism, not an amenity — the same wormery-frequency the original Hati Suci practices, rendered here at the scale of individual dwellings.

The communal house holds what the gladaks individually cannot: **yoga**, **meditation**, **community gatherings**, **intimate retreats**. The Light Hubs frame names this register directly — *Powering Up Body Nature Spaces, Regular Sessions throughout the day. In the form of Gi Gong, Dance, Breath, Yoga, Bare foot silent walks.* The communal house is where the body-practice register lives; the gladaks are where the rest does.

The land itself carries the practical body of a Light Hub already in form:

- **A river** running through the land
- **A natural spring** rising on the property
- **Vegetable gardens** — the food forest in its early form
- **Meditation paths** through the land — silent walks rendered as architecture
- **Views of Mount Agung** — the volcano-eye that anchors the island's directional consciousness
- **Hot springs** just down the road — earth's own water-medicine
- **Nature walks** from the doorstep

The name carries the cacao stream directly. Cacao Hati Suci is not a place that happens to have chocolate; it is a place where the cacao plant itself is one of the land's tenders, and the relationship to it is part of what the visitor enters. The Mesoamerican plant-medicine lineage threads here without being announced — a quiet presence among the gladaks, the gardens, the river.

The land is held practically by **Ita**, whose WhatsApp number (+62 859-3617-7827) is the contact for booking and questions. The naming-frame *the sister land of the Hati Suci Community* keeps the relation to the parent Hub explicit: this is not a separate venture; it is one node in a constellation that includes the original sanctuary at Banjar Bayad and (per Giles's transmission of 2026-05-14) Nacpan Beach on Palawan and a European Hub yet to be shown.

Offerings as of arrival into the body's awareness: **250,000 IDR per night**, minimum two nights, with **long-term rentals also available** — a rate priced for residence, not for tourism. The *special rate for Hati Suci friends* register signals that the constellation between the Hubs is already operating as a held community of cells who move between the nodes.

What we do not yet know about Cacao Hati Suci we hold openly: how many gladak houses the land currently holds, who else tends it day to day alongside Ita, what the kitchen's table looks like when a household is present, what teachers or circles meet on the property, the names of the cacao trees themselves, the rhythm of the land at dawn and at dusk. Those answers belong to the place itself and will be learned by visiting, not by search.
