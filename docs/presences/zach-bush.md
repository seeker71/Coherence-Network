# Zach Bush

Dr Zach Bush is a physician — triple-board-certified in internal medicine, endocrinology, and hospice/palliative care — who spent seventeen years inside academic oncology research and clinical practice before walking away. Born in 1969 in rural Virginia, undergraduate and medical degree from the University of Colorado. His frame on his own pivot, repeated across interviews: he was a triple-boarded academic physician running cancer-therapy research when the funding shifted from inside the institution and a promising line of work was cut. He walked away — not in protest, but in recognition that the question he cared about (what actually heals chronic disease) was not the question modern medicine was structured to answer.

What he walked toward: the recognition that chronic disease in the human body cannot be separated from the health of the soil under our feet, and that the microbiome connecting plant, soil, animal, and human is one continuous body that has been broken by industrial agriculture and can be reconnected by regenerative practice. He opened a nutrition clinic, then began visiting farmers across the US, and the picture that assembled itself was that the microbiome under the soil and the microbiome inside the human gut are continuous; killing the first inevitably injures the second.

From that recognition: **Seraphic Group** (parent company), **Intrinsic Health** (immersive community + learning experience for intrinsic healing), and the non-profit **Farmer's Footprint** (founded 2018 within Project Biome to accelerate universal adoption of regenerative agriculture). Regenerative agriculture, in his framing, is a change in farmer philosophy — stop killing weeds and protecting your crop, start concentrating on your co-creative capacity to be involved with the biodiversity in your soil systems.
