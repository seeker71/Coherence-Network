# Vali Soul Sanctuary

*The place that held the practice*

The sanctuary that turned a once-attended ceremonial floor into a regular practice. After the first [Pagan Ritual](/people/pagan-ritual) — the [Liquid Bloom](/people/liquid-bloom) room that became the threshold — Vali was the place where the body could keep coming back. Where ceremony stopped being a rare event and became *weekly ground*. Where [Tammy Beattie](/people/tammy-beattie) held [Ecstatic Movement Tribe](/people/ecstatic-movement-tribe), the room through which [Contact Improv](/people/contact-improv) entered. And alongside, many other divine gatherings that shaped this body.

## Grounding

- **Form** — Soul sanctuary · weekly ceremonial container · ecstatic-dance and conscious-movement venue · sacred space for embodied practice and community gathering
- **What it tends** — A continuous calendar of embodied-practice rooms — ecstatic dance, conscious movement, contact improv jams, sound journeys, breathwork, ceremony — held by a constellation of facilitators who tend their own practices inside the same ground. The sanctuary is the soil; the practices are the plants.
- **Function in this body's arc** — The continuity holder. After the threshold of the first [Pagan Ritual](/people/pagan-ritual), Vali was where the practice could keep being lived rather than only attended once. Without the sanctuary, the threshold would have been a singular memory; with it, the threshold became a doorway into a continuous body of weekly practice.
- **Lineage carried** — The Boulder transformational ecology — twenty-plus years deep — runs through Vali as one of its tendings. The same lineage that surfaces in the [Sunday-morning ballroom](/people/aly-constantine), in the floors that Shannon Lei Gill held in 2006 as Rhythm Sanctuary, in the festival rooms [Bloomurian](/people/bloomurian) opens, and in the cacao container [Mose](/people/mose) tends at Lake Atitlán. One field, many sanctuaries.
