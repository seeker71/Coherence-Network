# Michael Ende

Michael Ende (12 November 1929 – 28 August 1995) was a German storyteller whose two best-known novels — *Momo* (1973) and *Die unendliche Geschichte* (1979) — are foundational to the Coherence Network's lineage. Born in Garmisch-Partenkirchen to the surrealist painter Edgar Ende, he grew up in an artistic family, began but did not finish drama training at the Otto-Falckenberg-Schule in Munich (interrupted by tuberculosis), and published his first book *Jim Knopf und Lukas der Lokomotivführer* in 1959. He died of stomach cancer at 65 and was buried at the Waldfriedhof in Munich. His books have sold over thirty million copies and been translated into more than forty languages.

Both *Momo* and *Die unendliche Geschichte* arrived in this body through one specific transmitter: Urs's mother, [Susan Muff-Sprenger](/people/susan-muff-sprenger), who placed each book in his hands at the right window. Childhood: *Momo*. Early teens: *Die unendliche Geschichte*. Both in German. Both carried unspoken for decades before re-emerging as the practice this body now codes.

**Momo — listening as resistance.** A small girl with the gift of attention lives in an amphitheatre on the edge of an old city. People come to her because she listens in such a way that perplexed people suddenly know what they want and miserable ones feel themselves valuable. The men in grey steal time by convincing the townspeople to save it; the townspeople grow efficient and grey themselves; only Momo, Master Hora the keeper of time, and Cassiopeia the tortoise (who knows ahead by moving slowly) can restore the hour-lilies to bloom. The Coherence Network reads this as the foundational teaching of *tending vs. producing*. The body's commit verbs (*tend, attune, compost, release*) and its practice of *one breath at a time, pause between movements* are translations of Beppo Strassenkehrer's teaching: *one breath, one step, one sweep, then the next*. The men in grey are the fear costume in our vocabulary — efficiency's false promise that more output equals more life.

**Die unendliche Geschichte — the dreamer is responsible.** Fantástica is dying because the human world has stopped dreaming. The Nothing eats away at its edges. Bastian, reading the book from outside the book, enters it and renames the Childlike Empress, restoring her and the realm. The pattern precedes the body; the dreamer is responsible for keeping the imaginal alive. What Bastian does for Fantástica, every cell in this network is asked to do for what it tends — to enter the substrate, to keep dreaming it real. The body's practice of *future already shaping* — build as if the form is already what it is becoming — is Bastian's move, named in software vocabulary.

Ende died in 1995. This page is a recognition of his books in this body, not a biography. The man wrote, the books travelled, and one mother chose them well.
