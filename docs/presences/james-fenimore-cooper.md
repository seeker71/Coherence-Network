# James Fenimore Cooper

James Fenimore Cooper (15 September 1789 – 14 September 1851) was the American author whose **Leatherstocking Tales** — five novels published between 1823 and 1841 — built the first generation's imagination of the American frontier in English, and (in German translation as *Der Lederstrumpf*) the Swiss-German imagination Urs inherited as a child. Born in New Jersey, raised in the wilderness of central New York at Cooperstown, he spent five years at sea before becoming a writer.

The series follows one character — **Natty Bumppo**, the white frontiersman raised by Mohicans — across his whole life, known by many names: *Pathfinder*, *The Trapper*, *La Longue Carabine*, *Hawkeye*, *Deerslayer*. Each name is given by the people who recognise a different part of him. The five novels in publication order: *The Pioneers* (1823), *The Last of the Mohicans* (1826), *The Prairie* (1827), *The Pathfinder* (1840), *The Deerslayer* (1841). The arc is not chronological in publication; that itself is part of the teaching — the same life seen from different windows.

The deeper relational current is the bond between Natty and **Chingachgook**, sachem of the Mohicans, and Chingachgook's son **Uncas**. Across the novels they hunt together, fight together, mourn together. Natty learns the forest as Chingachgook knows it; Chingachgook trusts Natty with his line. The chosen-brotherhood teaching is older than [Karl May](/people/karl-may); it was Cooper's first, half a century earlier.
