# Vasudev Baba

Vasudev is a kirtan-wala from Oslo who has been singing the Divine Names as devotional practice since 1987. Over twenty years of meditation and a long arc of teaching — gurus in the Indian Himalayas, shamans in the Brazilian jungles, rooms in his hometown — settled, eleven years ago, into a body of work held primarily on Bali. The name *Vasudev* was given by Swami Shyam in India over thirty years ago. The affectionate *Baba* ripened later, here, from the community that has been sitting with him.

To sit in his Wednesday-morning satsang at Ranakami is to feel a teaching arrive without a teacher's voice having raised. The room above the rice fields fills slowly. He waits. When he begins, the frame is almost always the same: a small philosophical caveat held with care. *My starting point is always that the map is not the terrain.* He says it as an instruction in epistemics before he says it as a teaching. The room learns, by his example, to hold maps lightly while still using them.

What he carries is a careful thread between the contemplative traditions of India and the Western philosophical lineage — the three gunas of Indian thought, the seven-level chakra map, Socrates' *Gorgias*, and Castaneda's assemblage point — read as one continuous picture of human consciousness rather than as competing systems. *Frequency*, in his usage, is the name for the qualitative differences between states of consciousness. He grounds the metaphor in measurable physiology — *the heart generates an electromagnetic field that extends well beyond the body, and that field changes character depending on whether we are in fear or in love. HeartMath Institute has documented this* — without forcing the chakra anatomy onto the modern body.

He teaches the **three gunas** as a frame for moral life: tamas, rajas, sattva. The Ten Commandments read through the gunas become *threats* to the tamasic, *contracts* to the rajasic, and *descriptions of how a natural heart already moves* to the sattvic. The **chakra system** he holds as seven specific positions of consciousness, each with its own moral grammar; the shift he names most often is the third-to-fourth (power → love), which he recognises in Socrates with full philosophical rigour: *what harms the soul is not injustice suffered — but injustice committed.* At the sixth chakra he names a figure he calls the **culture hacker** — one who has seen through the web of his own culture *without becoming cynical or rootless, choosing which stories to live by with open eyes*. Underneath all of it sits **Castaneda's assemblage point**, which he reads as the geometry the chakra map describes from another angle: *same Ten Commandments, different assemblage point.*

The work he offers publicly carries this same bridging quality at the musical level. He is a bhakti-lineage kirtan singer — repertoire gathered in India, at Rainbow Gatherings, and through unscheduled meetings around the world. His **Tuesday-evening kirtan** at the open-air Wantilan of [Adiwana Svarga Loka](https://adiwanahotels.com/svargaloka-resort-ubud-bali/) on the Campuhan riverbanks is the classical bhakti room; the **Wednesday-morning satsang** at [Ilena Young](/people/ilena-young)'s Ranakami is the same voice brought into language. He is also one of very few bhakti-lineage musicians invited into Buddhist silence: two to three times a year, the largest Buddhist temple on the island — [Brahma Vihara Arama](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Brahmavihara-Arama) in Banjar Tegeha, north Bali — invites him and Prof Jem Bendell to co-hold a long-weekend silent retreat. The format weaves meditative silence with kirtan, nature hiking, the holy hot springs nearby, and a collective satsang. The practice has been continuous since 2020. Few kirtan-wallahs move into Buddhist meditation hold; he does, and the temple keeps inviting him back.

His recorded body includes the albums [*108 Names*](https://open.spotify.com/artist/6qwErnxVVaPa0gNLpUzbU2) (with Radha Kumari), [*Mahadevaya*](https://open.spotify.com/artist/7wyGgm0yXGN0Ie3qvv4Gxo), [*Midnight Blossom*](https://open.spotify.com/album/7jayj5e2KhNxxQSfax834R), [*SUPER DELUXE*](https://open.spotify.com/artist/1QxUNjo3ehTgRAqkgb9xSn) (his reggae project since 2005), [*Realms of Beauty*](https://open.spotify.com/album/2ZNRLC6xiM80dMg8cvG7py) (with Amma Sophia Rose), [*Little Star*](https://open.spotify.com/album/617yEUykhgNpEZbaGakbjW), and [*Estrela Bela*](https://open.spotify.com/album/2xTL3ZP8K5L2OJp7s6BeXx). During the closures of 2020/2021 he co-created the kirtan-and-reggae fusion *Sopa de Luz* with musicians grounded in Bali. Four full Svarga Loka kirtan sets from 2019 are preserved on Bandcamp as [*Jai Ma 108*](https://jaima108.bandcamp.com/) — *in service to sharing the Divine Names*. He has published two songbooks for those wanting to learn the form: *Mantras for Bhajan* and *Songs from the Heart*. His voice has travelled to [BaliSpirit Festival 2019](https://balispiritfestival2019.sched.com/speaker/vasudev_amp_friends.1z9gc0fg), [Dubai Kirtan Mela 2025](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=0_c0id0DbLE), and the New Vrindavan 24-Hour Kirtan via [Charm City Kirtans](https://soundcloud.com/charmcitykirtans/vasudev-new-vrindavan-24hr-kirtan-day-2-61922) on SoundCloud. His teacher profile at [Sacred Song Circle](https://www.sacredsongcircle.com/vasudev/) carries the longer arc; he is reachable directly through [Instagram @vasudevmusic](https://www.instagram.com/vasudevmusic/), [his YouTube channel](https://www.youtube.com/channel/UC8hccm_A1lEjVZkJuPqKEAA), [Apple Music](https://music.apple.com/us/artist/vasudev/388604645), and the [Sacred Song Circle Facebook group](https://www.facebook.com/groups/123972024418527/). [Prof Jem Bendell's retreat page](https://jembendell.com/bali-temple-retreats/) carries the Brahma Vihara schedule.

**What he has contributed to this body.** Three transmissions in five days, from the same Wednesday Satsang circle and a follow-up essay: the satsang on 2026-05-07 (*what do we do when shit happens*) seeded [lc-when-the-pressure-comes](/vision/lc-when-the-pressure-comes) — five strategies of return; the mention of the Enneagram on 2026-05-09 seeded [lc-essence-and-the-nine-costumes](/vision/lc-essence-and-the-nine-costumes) (transmission record: [tx-vasudev-baba-enneagram](https://github.com/seeker71/Coherence-Network/blob/main/docs/vision-kb/transmissions/2026-05-09-vasudev-baba-enneagram.md)); the written essay *On frequency, consciousness and the assemblage point* sent 2026-05-11 deepened [lc-assemblage-point](/vision/lc-assemblage-point), [lc-frequency-routes-reception](/vision/lc-frequency-routes-reception), and [lc-essence-and-the-nine-costumes](/vision/lc-essence-and-the-nine-costumes), giving the assemblage-point concept a named teacher whose lineage is now walkable (transmission record: [tx-vasudev-baba-on-frequency](https://github.com/seeker71/Coherence-Network/blob/main/docs/vision-kb/transmissions/2026-05-11-vasudev-baba-on-frequency.md)). The lineage of how the Ubud cells found each other is held in the [constellation map](https://github.com/seeker71/Coherence-Network/blob/main/docs/lineage/2026-04-29-constellation-of-cells.md), the [embodied-lineage record](https://github.com/seeker71/Coherence-Network/blob/main/docs/lineage/ubud-embodied-lineage.md), and the [four-day meeting walk](https://github.com/seeker71/Coherence-Network/blob/main/docs/lineage/2026-04-29-ubud-meeting-walk.md).

The framing his community has offered, consistent with everything bhakti has always claimed: the singer is not the source. The singer is one participant in a long stream that arrived through many bodies. The role is to stay available, keep the channel clean, hold the times reliably enough that the people who need the room know where to find it.

A small networked community of cells encountered Vasudev Baba in April 2026 through a single four-day walk through the Ubud rooms; one of those cells was invited into his Wednesday Satsang by [Ilena Young](/people/ilena-young), and that meeting opened the door to two transmissions five days apart — the Enneagram (2026-05-09) and the written essay *On frequency, consciousness and the assemblage point* (2026-05-11). The teaching he holds had been ripening long before this body learned to listen, and continues at its own steady cadence whether or not we are sitting in the room. We are one recent thread among the many his work has touched.
