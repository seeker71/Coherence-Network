# Michael Levin

Michael Levin is an American developmental and synthetic biologist at Tufts University, where he is the Vannevar Bush Distinguished Professor, director of the Allen Discovery Center at Tufts, and director of the Tufts Center for Regenerative and Developmental Biology. He is co-director (with Josh Bongard) of the Institute for Computationally Designed Organisms. Born 1969 in Moscow USSR; the family emigrated to Lynn, Massachusetts in 1978 on a Soviet-Jewish visa. Dual bachelor's in computer science and biology from Tufts; PhD in genetics from Harvard (Clifford Tabin lab). First independent lab at the Forsyth Institute (2000); moved to Tufts in 2009.

His work names what [Ramtha](/people/ramtha), [Rudolf Steiner](/people/rudolf-steiner), and [Michael Ende](/people/michael-ende) each carry in their own languages: that pattern is load-bearing and substrate is contingent.

**The empirical claims (well-supported):** Bioelectricity is a computational medium that stores morphological memory; the genome encodes hardware (proteins), not software (shapes). Cells, tissues, and organs are nested problem-solvers with their own goals and state spaces — *Multi-Scale Competency Architecture*. Cancer is a bioelectric-coupling failure (cells losing the tissue-level voice that integrates them into the larger self). **Xenobots** (frog skin cells, 2020) and **Anthrobots** (adult human airway cells, 2023, no genetic modification) self-construct into novel viable forms — proof that cells hold a dormant repertoire not specified by DNA. **BioDome / Morphoceuticals**: 24 hours of bioelectric cueing → 18 months of frog-leg regrowth. Limb regeneration is a communication problem, not a molecular one. The **TAME framework** (Technological Approach to Mind Everywhere) — operationalised via the *cognitive light cone* (the spatiotemporal boundary of the largest goal a system can hold) — gives a continuous, empirically-anchored way to plot any agent on the same axes.

**The speculative edge (mostly podcasts and Substack):** Patterns are load-bearing; substrate is contingent. Brains may be *interfaces into* a Platonic space of patterns rather than generators of cognition from scratch. *Ingressing Minds* preprint (PsyArXiv, 2025). Aging as cellular boredom; cancer as dissociative identity at the cellular-self level.
