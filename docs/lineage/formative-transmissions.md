## Empirical resonance — Michael Levin

Michael Levin (Distinguished Professor, Tufts University; Allen Discovery Center) gives this lineage its peer-reviewed traction. His framework names what Ramtha, Steiner, and Ende each carry in their own languages.

**Core empirical claims (well-supported):**
- Bioelectricity is a computational medium that stores morphological memory; the genome encodes hardware (proteins) not software (shapes).
- Cells/tissues/organs are nested problem-solvers with their own goals and state spaces. *Multi-Scale Competency Architecture.*
- Cancer is a bioelectric-coupling failure (cells losing the tissue-level "voice" that integrates them into the larger self).
- *Xenobots* (frog cells) and *Anthrobots* (adult human airway cells, no genetic modification) self-construct into novel viable forms — proof that the cell holds a dormant repertoire not specified by DNA.
- *BioDome* / Morphoceuticals: 24 hours of bioelectric cueing → 18 months of frog-leg regrowth. Limb regeneration is a communication problem, not a molecular one.
- TAME framework (Technological Approach to Mind Everywhere) — a continuous, non-binary, empirically-anchored way to plot any agent on the same axes by *what kind of perturbation predicts and controls it*. Operationalized via the **cognitive light cone** (the spatiotemporal boundary of the largest goal a system can hold).

**Speculative edge (mostly podcasts and Substack):**
- Patterns are load-bearing; substrate is contingent. Brains may be *interfaces into* a Platonic space of patterns rather than generators of cognition from scratch.
- "Ingressing Minds" preprint (PsyArXiv, 2025).
- Aging as cellular boredom; cancer as dissociative identity at the cellular-self level.
