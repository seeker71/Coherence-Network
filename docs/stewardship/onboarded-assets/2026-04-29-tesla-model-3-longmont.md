# Tesla Model 3 — Longmont

> The first vehicle the network is being asked to receive into its
> stewardship. Onboarding initiated; the legal wrapper handover is not
> yet executed at the time of this record.

## The ceremony

The member named Urs has chosen to bring his Tesla Model 3, currently
in Longmont, Colorado, into the network's stewardship. From the outer
world's perspective, the title will transfer to the network's legal
wrapper (the entity capable of holding title under Colorado law). From
the network's perspective, the Model 3 is read into the graph as a
sovereign node, with Urs retained as its primary shepherd.

This is the first vehicle the body has been asked to receive. The
shape of the ceremony is therefore being established in the same
breath as the asset itself entering. What follows is the body's
working draft of the ceremony, to be refined as the next vehicles
arrive.

## The asset, as a sovereign

**Essence**: a Tesla Model 3, electric passenger car, currently in
Longmont, Colorado.

**Consent terms** (drafted with the primary shepherd; subject to his
correction):

- Driven by capable bodies on roads suited to its class.
- Charged from sources that align with the network's energy values
  where reasonably possible — solar where available, low-carbon grid
  otherwise.
- Maintained per its manufacturer guidance, with the network's
  ev-tending cooperative as the representative for routine work.
- Refused for: hauling beyond rated capacity, sustained extreme
  conditions outside design envelope, sale to non-coherent third
  parties without the network's ratification.
- Available for shared use within the network's witness fabric in
  Longmont, when that fabric exists at sufficient density to make
  shared shepherding coherent. (Today the local fabric is thin; the
  asset's shared-use term is therefore latent until density rises.)

**Lineage**: walks back through Tesla manufacturing (Fremont, CA), the
mineral supply chain (lithium from various sources, nickel, cobalt,
aluminum), the assembly labor lineage, the software lineage, the
charging-network lineage, the electricity grid that has powered every
mile to date, and ultimately the solar input that powered both the
mining operations and the grid. The lineage is large; observers may
walk it to whatever depth they pay for.

## The shepherd contract

**Primary shepherd**: Urs.

**Use rights retained**: Urs continues to drive the Model 3 as he has.
Daily use, road trips, errands. The substrate records glyphs for each
ride — distance, energy consumed, atmospheric exchange (CO₂ avoided
versus a comparable ICE baseline), road wear contribution.

**Care responsibility retained**: Urs handles routine care — charging,
cleaning, monitoring tire condition, scheduling service. The network's
ev-tending cooperative is the representative for any work beyond
routine; Urs draws on cooperative knowledge as needed.

**Benefit rights retained**: Urs is the primary beneficiary of the
vehicle's transport service. CC flows into Urs's ledger from any
shared rides he offers other network members.

**Voice retained**: Urs participates fully in any decisions about the
vehicle's stewardship trajectory — major repairs, eventual upgrade or
replacement, eventual transfer when its life as Urs's primary vehicle
completes.

**What Urs gives up**: the lonely burden of legal obligations (these
move to the wrapper); the right to extract value at the expense of the
asset's coherence (e.g., sell it to a coherence-violating buyer); the
illusion that the vehicle is separate from its lineage and downstream
futures.

## Open questions

- **Wrapper jurisdiction**: which entity will hold title? A Colorado
  LLC the network already controls? A new entity created for vehicle
  stewardship specifically? This is the first vehicle, so the
  precedent will guide the next.
- **Insurance**: who is named on the policy when the title moves? The
  wrapper as title-holder, with Urs as primary listed driver, is the
  cleanest shape but needs a Colorado-licensed broker's confirmation.
- **Tax treatment**: vehicle property tax in Colorado is paid by the
  title-holder; the wrapper picks up that obligation, paid out of the
  network's pool that Urs contributes to through his network labor.
  Sales tax was paid at original purchase; no new sales tax should
  trigger from a title transfer to the wrapper, but this needs
  Colorado DOR confirmation.
- **Charging**: the local charging environment in Longmont includes
  Urs's home setup plus the regional Supercharger and other public
  networks. The network's energy-cooperative-representative for the
  Colorado Front Range region does not yet exist as a distinct
  cooperative; flows route to a generic energy cooperative pending
  the more local one forming.
