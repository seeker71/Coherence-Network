# Knowledge and Resonance

The semantic substrate. Coherence scoring, idea matching, belief translation, and serendipity engines all depend on a shared vocabulary — a layered ontology where concepts have relationships, ideas have resonance with concepts, and contributors have belief systems. This is the layer that makes the platform "know what it's talking about."

## Problem

Without a concept layer, every idea is a bag of words. Similarity is keyword matching, prioritization is vibes, and cross-domain resonance is invisible. We need a living ontology where 184 universal concepts connect via 46 typed relationships, ideas attract related ideas across domains, and contributors can see any idea through their own belief system. This is not abstract research — it is what makes coherence scoring meaningful.

## Key Capabilities

- **Concept layer foundation**: 184 universal concepts, 46 relationship types, 53 axes from the Living Codex ontology. Concepts are nodes; relationships are edges. Provides the vocabulary for coherence scoring.
- **Concept resonance kernel**: Harmonic similarity matching — two ideas that share underlying concepts resonate even when surface words differ. Enables cross-domain discovery.
- **Belief system interface**: Per-contributor worldview, interests, concept preferences. Two contributors can see the same idea through different frames.
- **Belief translation**: Translate any idea through any worldview — same content, different frame. Reduces barrier to entry for non-technical contributors.
- **Ice / Water / Gas phase states**: Knowledge has states — crystallized (ice), flowing (water), exploratory (gas). Transitions are explicit and reversible.
- **Breath cycle**: Compose → expand → validate → contract → release → rest. Every concept and idea follows this lifecycle, atomically.
- **Fractal zoom navigation**: Navigate the knowledge graph at any scale. Same patterns recur at every level — idea, spec, task, contribution.
- **Temporal consciousness**: Time-aware exploration with causality tracking. What caused what, when, and what followed.
- **Meta-nodes / self-description**: The graph describes itself. Code and API are introspectable via the same concept layer.

## What Success Looks Like

- Every idea has a concept vector (which of the 184 concepts it touches)
- Resonance queries return semantically related ideas across domains, not just keyword matches
- A contributor can flip a "worldview" switch and see the ideas page re-ranked for their belief system
- The concept layer is versioned, signed, and federated alongside ideas
